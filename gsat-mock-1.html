第一部分　詞彙題（第1-10題）

1.The mountain climber showed great ______ by continuing her ascent despite the freezing temperatures.

(A) resilience
(B) indifference
(C) hesitation
(D) convenience
中譯：那位登山者即使在嚴寒的氣溫下仍持續攻頂，展現了極大的＿＿＿。 正解 (A) resilience（韌性、堅毅）：句意指即使在惡劣的天候條件下仍堅持向上攀登，正符合「堅毅、不屈不撓」之意，與 despite the freezing temperatures（即使在嚴寒下）呼應。 (B) indifference（冷漠、不在乎）：冷漠是一種消極、不在意的態度，與「持續攻頂」展現出的積極堅持精神相矛盾，不合句意。 (C) hesitation（猶豫）：猶豫表示遲疑不決，但句中描述的是「continuing her ascent」（持續攀登）這一持續且果斷的動作，與猶豫不決的語意相反。 (D) convenience（便利、方便）：便利指事物容易、方便的特性，與登山者在艱困環境下展現的精神品質完全無關，語意不符。

2.The documentary film ______ the harsh living conditions faced by people in remote areas of the world.

(A) celebrated
(B) portrayed
(C) disguised
(D) overlooked
中譯：這部紀錄片＿＿＿了世界偏遠地區人們所面臨的嚴苛生活條件。 正解 (B) portrayed（描繪、刻劃）：portray 常用於描述影片、文章或藝術作品「描繪、呈現」某種情況或人物，與 documentary film（紀錄片）描繪 harsh living conditions（嚴苛的生活條件）的語境完全吻合。 (A) celebrated（讚揚、慶祝）：celebrate 帶有正面、頌揚的語氣，但 harsh living conditions 是負面、艱困的處境，紀錄片通常是「呈現」而非「讚揚」這類困境，語意不符。 (C) disguised（偽裝、掩飾）：disguise 表示刻意隱藏真相、偽裝成別的樣子，但紀錄片的功能正是「揭露真實」，與此處語意相反。 (D) overlooked（忽視、忽略）：overlook 表示沒有注意到或刻意不理會，但句子說紀錄片正是聚焦呈現這些生活條件，並非忽視它們，語意矛盾。

3.The teacher asked students to ______ the main ideas of the article in their own words.

(A) elaborate
(B) memorize
(C) summarize
(D) translate
中譯：老師要求學生用自己的話＿＿＿這篇文章的主要觀點。 正解 (C) summarize（總結、概述）：summarize 指用較少的文字將重點濃縮表達出來，與「用自己的話表達 main ideas（主要觀點）」的語境最為契合，這正是摘要寫作的定義。 (A) elaborate（詳細闡述）：elaborate 表示對某事做更詳盡、更深入的說明，與「用自己的話表達主旨」這種濃縮、精簡的要求方向相反。 (B) memorize（記憶、背誦）：memorize 指單純地把內容背下來，並不涉及「用自己的話」重新表達，與句意不符。 (D) translate（翻譯）：translate 是將一種語言轉換成另一種語言，文中並未提及語言轉換的情境，與「用自己的話表達主旨」的要求不同。

4.The patient recovery of the injured athlete was seen as nothing short of ______.

(A) miraculous
(B) predictable
(C) reluctant
(D) temporary
中譯：那位受傷運動員的康復過程被認為簡直是＿＿＿的。 正解 (A) miraculous（奇蹟般的）：nothing short of 是「簡直就是、不亞於」的意思，用來加強語氣，表示康復的程度超乎預期、令人驚奇，與 miraculous（奇蹟般的）搭配最為合理，符合對「驚人康復」的讚嘆語氣。 (B) predictable（可預測的）：predictable 表示事情符合預期、毫不意外，但「nothing short of + 形容詞」通常用於形容超乎尋常、令人驚訝之事，與「可預測」的平淡語意矛盾。 (C) reluctant（不情願的）：reluctant 是用來形容「人」對某事的不情願態度，無法用來形容「recovery（復原過程）」這個抽象名詞的特性，語法搭配不當。 (D) temporary（暫時的）：temporary 表示短暫、非永久性，與「nothing short of」所強調的驚人程度無關，且與康復這個正向結果的語境不搭。

5.As competition in the market grew fiercer, the company was forced to ______ its prices.

(A) multiply
(B) stabilize
(C) exaggerate
(D) slash
中譯：隨著市場競爭日益激烈，這家公司被迫＿＿＿其價格。 正解 (D) slash（大幅削減）：slash 常用於描述「大幅度地削減」價格、預算等，與 competition in the market grew fiercer（市場競爭愈發激烈）導致公司必須降價以求競爭的邏輯完全吻合。 (A) multiply（使倍增）：multiply 表示數量大幅增加，但在競爭激烈時提高價格通常會讓公司失去競爭力，與邏輯相反。 (B) stabilize（使穩定）：stabilize 表示維持價格不變、使其穩定，但句子強調競爭「愈發激烈」迫使公司採取行動，stabilize 無法呈現「被迫」做出改變的緊迫感。 (C) exaggerate（誇大）：exaggerate 通常用於描述言語、說法的誇大，不會用來搭配「prices（價格）」這個受詞，搭配不當且語意不符。

6.The government has launched a new campaign to ______ citizens about the dangers of smoking.

(A) educate
(B) entertain
(C) distract
(D) manipulate
中譯：政府已經發起一項新的宣傳活動，以＿＿＿民眾關於吸菸的危害。 正解 (A) educate（教育、教導）：educate citizens about the dangers of smoking（教育民眾關於吸菸的危害）語意通順，campaign（宣傳活動）的常見目的便是提供資訊、教育大眾，與句意完全吻合。 (B) entertain（娛樂）：entertain 表示提供娛樂消遣，與政府宣傳「吸菸危害」這種具警示性、教育性質的內容方向不符。 (C) distract（使分心）：distract 表示讓人分心、轉移注意力，這與政府想要讓民眾「關注並了解」吸菸危害的目的恰好相反。 (D) manipulate（操控）：manipulate 帶有負面的「操弄、欺騙」意味，與政府宣導健康知識這種正面、公益性質的行動語氣不符。

7.The chef's ______ use of local ingredients gave his dishes a unique and authentic flavor.

(A) excessive
(B) creative
(C) reluctant
(D) absent
中譯：這位主廚對當地食材＿＿＿的運用，賦予他的菜餚一種獨特而道地的風味。 正解 (B) creative（有創意的）：creative use of local ingredients（有創意地運用當地食材）能合理解釋為何菜餚會有 unique and authentic flavor（獨特又道地的風味），創意是產生獨特成果的關鍵。 (A) excessive（過度的）：excessive 表示「過量、過度」，過度使用食材並不必然產生「獨特道地的風味」，且 excessive 帶有負面意味，與整句讚揚主廚的語氣不符。 (C) reluctant（不情願的）：reluctant 用於形容人對某事不情願的態度，不能直接修飾「use（使用）」這個名詞所帶出的動作特質，且不情願的使用方式難以產生佳評。 (D) absent（缺席的、不存在的）：absent use 在語法及語意上皆不通，「不存在的使用」邏輯矛盾，無法解釋風味的產生。

8.The scientist's groundbreaking research ______ new possibilities for the treatment of cancer.

(A) closed off
(B) looked into
(C) opened up
(D) passed over
中譯：這位科學家突破性的研究為癌症治療＿＿＿了新的可能性。 正解 (C) opened up（開創、打開）：open up new possibilities（開創新的可能性）是固定且常見的搭配，意指帶來新的機會或方向，與 groundbreaking research（突破性研究）帶來癌症治療新希望的語境完全吻合。 (A) closed off（關閉、阻斷）：closed off new possibilities 語意矛盾，「阻斷新的可能性」與「突破性研究」帶來正向發展的語境相反。 (B) looked into（調查、研究）：look into 表示「調查、探究」，其受詞通常是問題或議題本身，而非「possibilities（可能性）」這種抽象結果，搭配不自然。 (D) passed over（略過、忽視）：pass over 表示忽略、不予理會，與研究「帶來新可能性」這種積極正向的結果完全相反。

9.The committee decided to ______ the proposal after careful review and extensive discussion.

(A) adopt
(B) ignore
(C) postpone
(D) replace
中譯：經過仔細審查與廣泛討論後，委員會決定＿＿＿這項提案。 正解 (A) adopt（採納、採用）：after careful review and extensive discussion（經過仔細審查與廣泛討論）暗示經過慎重考慮後做出正面決定，adopt the proposal（採納提案）符合「審慎評估後接受」的邏輯。 (B) ignore（忽視）：如果委員會經過 careful review and extensive discussion（仔細審查與廣泛討論），就不太可能是「忽視」這項提案，邏輯矛盾。 (C) postpone（延後）：postpone 表示延後決定，但句子強調「經過審慎討論後」做出的是一個明確的決定（decided to...），而非延後決定的行為，語意較不搭。 (D) replace（取代）：replace the proposal 表示用別的東西取代這項提案，但句子並未提及有其他替代方案存在，且與「經過審查討論後的決定」邏輯不直接相關。

10.Despite the ______ weather conditions, the outdoor concert was a massive success.

(A) favorable
(B) predictable
(C) adverse
(D) pleasant
中譯：儘管天氣＿＿＿，這場戶外音樂會仍取得了巨大的成功。 正解 (C) adverse（不利的、惡劣的）：despite（儘管）後面通常接與主句結果相反或形成對比的情況，adverse weather conditions（惡劣的天氣條件）與後面「concert was a massive success（音樂會大獲成功）」形成「儘管不利、仍然成功」的合理對比。 (A) favorable（有利的）：若天氣是 favorable（有利的），音樂會成功便是理所當然，與 despite（儘管）所暗示的「逆境中仍成功」的轉折語氣不符。 (B) predictable（可預測的）：天氣「可預測」與音樂會是否成功沒有直接的因果或對比關係，無法與 despite 構成合理的語意轉折。 (D) pleasant（舒適宜人的）：若天氣 pleasant（宜人），與後面的成功結果並無對比性，despite 所需要的「逆境—成功」對比關係無法成立。

第11至15題為題組

Every language has its own set of idioms—expressions whose meanings cannot be understood from the individual words alone. In English, for example, saying someone "kicked the bucket" 11 the person has died. Such expressions can be puzzling for language learners, as their meanings are rarely 12 from the literal words used. Idioms often reflect the history, culture, and values of the people who speak a language. They may arise from everyday activities, professions, or historical events that were once 13 to a large number of people. Over time, as society changes, some idioms become outdated and fall out of use, 14 new ones emerge to reflect contemporary life. For instance, expressions related to the internet and technology have become widespread in recent years. Understanding idioms is 15 for anyone who wants to truly master a foreign language, since native speakers use them constantly in both casual and formal speech.

第35至38題為題組

The concept of "slow food" was born in Italy in 1989 as a direct response to the opening of a McDonald's restaurant near the Spanish Steps in Rome. Founded by journalist Carlo Petrini, the Slow Food movement began as a protest against fast food culture and has since grown into an international organization with over 100,000 members in more than 160 countries. The movement advocates for traditional recipes, local ingredients, and sustainable farming practices. At its heart, slow food is about taking time to appreciate where food comes from, how it is produced, and who grows it. Slow food promotes three core principles: good, clean, and fair. "Good" refers to food that is fresh and flavorful. "Clean" means food produced in ways that are not harmful to the environment or human health. "Fair" emphasizes fair prices for consumers and fair wages for producers. These principles stand in stark contrast to the efficiency-driven logic of industrial food production, which often prioritizes cost reduction and uniformity over quality and sustainability. One of the most celebrated programs of the Slow Food movement is the Ark of Taste, a catalog of over 5,000 forgotten or endangered foods from around the world. From rare varieties of apples in the UK to traditional cheeses in the mountains of Switzerland, the Ark of Taste aims to preserve culinary biodiversity. Local chapters, called "convivia," organize events such as farmers' markets, cooking workshops, and farm visits to reconnect communities with their food heritage. Critics argue that slow food is a luxury for the wealthy, since high-quality local produce is often more expensive than mass-produced alternatives. Supporters counter that the true cost of cheap food is hidden in environmental damage, health consequences, and the exploitation of agricultural workers. Regardless of one's position, the Slow Food movement has successfully sparked a global conversation about the values embedded in what and how we eat.

In the animal kingdom, cooperation between different species—known as mutualism—is surprisingly common. One of the most well-known examples is the relationship between clownfish and sea anemones. The sea anemone's tentacles are venomous to most fish, but the clownfish has a special mucus coating on its skin that protects it from the sting. In exchange for shelter among the anemone's tentacles, the clownfish drives away parasites and small fish that might feed on the anemone. Both parties benefit: the clownfish gets protection, and the anemone gets cleaning services and nutrients from the clownfish's waste. Another remarkable example is the oxpecker bird and large African mammals such as zebras, rhinos, and giraffes. Oxpeckers spend their days perched on the backs and necks of these large animals, feeding on ticks, flies, and other parasites. The mammals receive a free grooming service that helps keep them healthy, while the birds enjoy a steady food source. Additionally, oxpeckers serve as an alarm system—their sharp calls warn host animals when predators are approaching. Perhaps the most ecologically significant mutualistic relationship is the one between plants and their pollinators. Bees, butterflies, hummingbirds, and even some bats visit flowers to collect nectar and pollen for food. In doing so, they inadvertently transfer pollen from one flower to another, enabling plant reproduction. This relationship is so fundamental that the decline of pollinators, particularly bees, has raised serious concerns among scientists and farmers alike. Without pollinators, many crops would fail, threatening global food security. What these examples illustrate is a broader principle: in nature, self-interest and mutual benefit are not always in conflict. Evolution has repeatedly produced arrangements where helping others is the most effective survival strategy.

Every four years, athletes from around the world gather to compete in the Olympic Games—a tradition that dates back to ancient Greece. The original games were held in Olympia and dedicated to Zeus, the king of the Greek gods. They took place from approximately 776 BC until 393 AD, when the Roman Emperor Theodosius I abolished them as part of a campaign to eliminate pagan practices. The modern Olympic movement was revived in the late 19th century, largely through the efforts of French educator Pierre de Coubertin. Inspired by the ancient games, Coubertin believed that athletic competition could promote international understanding and peace. The first modern Olympic Games were held in Athens, Greece, in 1896, with 14 nations and 241 athletes participating. Today, the Summer Olympics attract over 10,000 athletes from more than 200 countries, making it the world's largest international sporting event. Throughout their history, the Olympics have often been caught up in political controversies. The 1936 Games in Berlin were overshadowed by the Nazi regime's attempt to use them as a propaganda tool. The 1980 Moscow Olympics saw a boycott by 65 nations in protest of the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan. The following year, the Soviet Union and its allies boycotted the 1984 Los Angeles Games in retaliation. Despite these controversies, the Olympic spirit endures. The Olympic motto, "Faster, Higher, Stronger—Together," added the word "Together" in 2021 to reflect the spirit of unity in the face of global challenges. The Games continue to provide a stage where athletic excellence and human achievement are celebrated, reminding the world that competition and cooperation are not mutually exclusive.
