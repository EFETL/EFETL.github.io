第一部分　詞彙題（第1-10題）

1.The factory was fined for releasing toxic waste that ______ the nearby river for years.

(A) contaminated
(B) decorated
(C) illuminated
(D) compensated
中譯：這家工廠因排放毒性廢棄物，多年來＿＿＿了附近的河流，而遭到罰款。 正解 (A) contaminated（污染）：contaminate 指「污染、弄髒」，與 toxic waste（毒性廢棄物）污染 nearby river（附近河流）的語境完全吻合，工廠因此遭罰也合乎邏輯。 (B) decorated（裝飾）：decorate 指美化、裝飾，與「排放毒性廢棄物」造成的負面後果語意相反，且工廠不會因「裝飾河流」遭罰。 (C) illuminated（照亮）：illuminate 指照亮、使明亮，與「毒性廢棄物」對河流造成的破壞性影響毫無關聯。 (D) compensated（補償）：compensate 指補償、賠償，通常用於人或損失，「補償河流」搭配不自然，且與「排放毒性廢棄物」的因果關係不符——廢棄物造成的是污染而非補償。

2.Although the new policy was controversial, the mayor remained ______ in his decision and refused to back down.

(A) reluctant
(B) adequate
(C) firm
(D) feeble
中譯：儘管新政策引發爭議，市長在他的決定上仍保持＿＿＿，拒絕讓步。 正解 (C) firm（堅定的）：remain firm in his decision（在決定上保持堅定）與後文 refused to back down（拒絕讓步）語意一致，皆強調市長態度堅決、不為爭議所動搖。 (A) reluctant（不情願的）：reluctant 表示猶豫、不情願，但句子強調市長「拒絕讓步」，顯示他態度堅定而非猶豫不決，語意矛盾。 (B) adequate（足夠的）：adequate 指數量或品質上「足夠」，無法用來形容一個人在決定上的態度，搭配不當。 (D) feeble（軟弱無力的）：feeble 表示軟弱、無力，與「拒絕讓步」展現的堅定態度完全相反。

3.The scientist's discovery was so ______ that it completely changed how we understand the origins of the universe.

(A) trivial
(B) groundbreaking
(C) ordinary
(D) familiar
中譯：這位科學家的發現是如此＿＿＿，徹底改變了我們對宇宙起源的理解。 正解 (B) groundbreaking（突破性的）：groundbreaking discovery（突破性的發現）能合理解釋為何「徹底改變了我們對宇宙起源的理解」，因為突破性的發現正是足以顛覆既有認知的重大成果。 (A) trivial（微不足道的）：trivial 表示瑣碎、不重要，與「徹底改變了我們對宇宙起源的理解」這種重大影響完全矛盾。 (C) ordinary（普通的）：ordinary 表示平凡、普通，無法解釋「徹底改變理解」這種非凡的結果，邏輯不符。 (D) familiar（熟悉的）：familiar 表示為人熟知、不陌生，但句子強調這項發現帶來「改變理解」的衝擊性，與「熟悉」的平淡語意不符。

4.Volunteers worked together to ______ the abandoned park, planting flowers and repairing the benches.

(A) revive
(B) abandon
(C) neglect
(D) postpone
中譯：志工們齊心協力＿＿＿這座被遺棄的公園，種花並修補長椅。 正解 (A) revive（使重生、復甦）：revive the abandoned park（讓被遺棄的公園重獲生機）與後文「種花、修補長椅」的具體行動相呼應，皆是為了讓荒廢的公園恢復活力，語意通順。 (B) abandon（遺棄）：abandon 表示放棄、遺棄，但句子已說明這座公園是「abandoned」（已被遺棄的），志工們的行動是要改善它，而非再次遺棄它，語意矛盾。 (C) neglect（忽視、疏於照顧）：neglect 表示忽略、不予理會，與志工們「種花、修補長椅」的積極行動完全相反。 (D) postpone（延後）：postpone 表示延後進行，無法用來描述志工們對公園採取的具體「修復」行動，語意不符。

5.The negotiations between the two companies broke down because neither side was willing to ______.

(A) compromise
(B) celebrate
(C) exaggerate
(D) hesitate
中譯：這兩家公司之間的談判破裂了，因為雙方都不願意＿＿＿。 正解 (A) compromise（妥協、讓步）：談判破裂(broke down)的常見原因正是「雙方都不願妥協」(neither side was willing to compromise)，compromise 與「談判破裂」的因果邏輯完全吻合。 (B) celebrate（慶祝）：celebrate 表示慶祝，與「談判破裂」這種負面結果毫無邏輯關聯。 (C) exaggerate（誇大）：exaggerate 表示誇大其詞，與「談判破裂」的原因（雙方立場僵持不下）無直接因果關係。 (D) hesitate（猶豫）：hesitate 表示猶豫不決，但「不願意猶豫」並不會導致談判破裂，事實上猶豫反而可能延緩破裂，邏輯不符。

6.Despite repeated warnings, the hikers chose to ______ the danger and continued climbing during the storm.

(A) acknowledge
(B) ignore
(C) embrace
(D) announce
中譯：儘管再三警告，登山客仍選擇＿＿＿這項危險，在風暴中繼續攀登。 正解 (B) ignore（忽視）：despite repeated warnings（儘管再三警告）後面接「登山客選擇忽視危險」繼續攀登，ignore the danger（忽視危險）與「儘管警告」形成合理的轉折，描述登山客不顧勸告的行為。 (A) acknowledge（承認、認知到）：若登山客「承認」了危險，卻仍「continued climbing during the storm」（在風暴中繼續攀登），語意上較不通順——acknowledge 暗示已意識到風險，但句子強調的是「不顧警告」的態度，ignore 更符合「不顧勸告」的語境。 (C) embrace（擁抱、欣然接受）：embrace the danger（欣然接受危險）語氣過於正面、主動追求危險，與「儘管再三警告」這種勸阻的語境搭配不自然，不如 ignore（忽視警告）合理。 (D) announce（宣布）：announce the danger（宣布危險）語意不通，登山客的行為是「無視警告繼續攀登」，並非「宣布危險」這個動作。

7.The museum's new exhibit aims to ______ visitors about the history of ancient trade routes through interactive displays.

(A) enlighten
(B) confuse
(C) restrict
(D) discourage
中譯：這間博物館的新展覽旨在透過互動展示，向參觀者＿＿＿關於古代貿易路線的歷史。 正解 (A) enlighten（啟發、教導）：enlighten visitors about the history（向參觀者講解歷史知識，使其有所了解）與 museum's new exhibit（博物館新展覽）透過 interactive displays（互動展示）達成的教育目的完全吻合。 (B) confuse（使困惑）：confuse visitors（使參觀者困惑）與博物館展覽「旨在」（aims to）達成的教育性正面目的相反，展覽的設計目標不會是讓人困惑。 (C) restrict（限制）：restrict visitors（限制參觀者）語意不通，「限制參觀者關於古代貿易路線的歷史」邏輯不合，restrict 通常搭配具體的限制對象（如進入、活動等），非「關於歷史知識」。 (D) discourage（使沮喪、勸阻）：discourage visitors about history（使參觀者對歷史感到沮喪）與展覽透過互動方式吸引參觀者學習的正面目的相反。

8.After months of training, the team's performance showed a ______ improvement compared to last season.

(A) negligible
(B) marginal
(C) remarkable
(D) doubtful
中譯：經過數月的訓練後，這支隊伍的表現相比上一季顯示出＿＿＿的進步。 正解 (C) remarkable（顯著的、卓越的）：remarkable improvement（顯著的進步）與「經過數月訓練」的努力成果相呼應，表示訓練帶來了明顯且值得注意的進步，語意正向且合理。 (A) negligible（微不足道的）：negligible improvement（微不足道的進步）與「經過數月訓練」所應帶來的成效不成比例，若進步微不足道，便難以凸顯訓練的價值，雖文法可通但語氣與上下文強調訓練成果的方向較不搭。 (B) marginal（些微的）：marginal improvement（些微的進步）同樣暗示進步幅度很小，與「數月訓練」應有的顯著成效不符，語氣較弱。 (D) doubtful（可疑的、不確定的）：doubtful improvement（可疑的進步）語意不通，「進步」本身是一個事實陳述，用「可疑的」來形容「進步」邏輯矛盾——若進步本身就「可疑」，便不能稱之為「進步」。

9.The committee decided to ______ the meeting until all the necessary documents had been reviewed.

(A) postpone
(B) accelerate
(C) finalize
(D) publicize
中譯：委員會決定＿＿＿這場會議，直到所有必要的文件都被審查完畢。 正解 (A) postpone（延後）：postpone the meeting until all the necessary documents had been reviewed（將會議延後至所有文件都審查完畢）語意通順，until（直到）所引導的條件子句正好說明「延後」的原因與期限，語法與語意皆吻合。 (B) accelerate（加速）：accelerate the meeting until...（將會議加速直到文件審查完畢）語意不通，「加速會議」與「等待文件審查完畢」這個需要時間的前提相互矛盾。 (C) finalize（完成、確定）：finalize the meeting until...（將會議定案直到文件審查完畢）語法搭配不自然，finalize 通常指「敲定、完成某事」，與 until 子句所暗示「尚未完成、需等待」的語境不一致。 (D) publicize（公開宣傳）：publicize the meeting until...（公開宣傳會議直到文件審查完畢）語意不通，「公開宣傳會議」與「等待文件審查完畢」之間沒有合理的邏輯關聯。

10.The novel's plot is so ______ that readers often need to take notes to keep track of all the characters.

(A) straightforward
(B) intricate
(C) shallow
(D) monotonous
中譯：這部小說的情節如此＿＿＿，讀者經常需要做筆記才能跟上所有角色的脈絡。 正解 (B) intricate（複雜精細的）：intricate plot（錯綜複雜的情節）能合理解釋為何「讀者需要做筆記才能跟上所有角色」(readers often need to take notes to keep track of all the characters)，情節越複雜，越需要額外的輔助來理解。 (A) straightforward（直接易懂的）：straightforward plot（簡單明瞭的情節）與「讀者需要做筆記才能跟上角色」的情況相反——若情節簡單明瞭，就不需要額外做筆記輔助理解。 (C) shallow（膚淺的）：shallow plot（膚淺的情節）通常指情節缺乏深度，這與「讀者需要做筆記才能跟上所有角色」所暗示的「情節複雜、角色眾多」的語境不符。 (D) monotonous（單調的）：monotonous plot（單調乏味的情節）表示情節一成不變、缺乏變化，與「需要做筆記跟上眾多角色」所暗示的「內容豐富、複雜」的情況相反。

第35至38題為題組

Long before modern highways and airplanes connected the world, a vast network of trade routes known as the Silk Road linked East Asia with the Mediterranean. Stretching over 6,000 kilometers, the Silk Road was not a single road but a web of overland and maritime paths used by merchants, travelers, and pilgrims for more than a thousand years. The name "Silk Road" comes from the valuable silk fabric that traveled westward from China, where the technique of silk production was kept a closely guarded secret for centuries. In exchange, goods such as glassware, spices, precious stones, and horses moved eastward. However, the Silk Road carried far more than physical goods. Ideas, religions, languages, and even diseases traveled along these routes, transforming the cultures of the regions they passed through. Buddhism, for example, spread from India into Central Asia and China largely through monks who traveled along the Silk Road, carrying scriptures and establishing monasteries at oasis towns. Similarly, technologies such as papermaking and gunpowder, both invented in China, eventually reached Europe through these trade networks, sometimes taking centuries to make the journey. Life along the Silk Road was far from easy. Merchants faced harsh deserts, towering mountain passes, bandits, and unpredictable weather. Caravans of camels, often numbering in the hundreds, carried goods between oasis cities that served as crucial resting points, offering food, water, and lodging to weary travelers. The Silk Road gradually declined in importance after the 15th century, as sea routes around Africa became safer and more efficient for transporting goods between Europe and Asia. Today, the legacy of the Silk Road lives on, not only in historical artifacts and ancient cities but also in the modern "Belt and Road Initiative," a large infrastructure project that some have described as an attempt to revive the spirit of this ancient network.

Gene editing, once confined to science fiction, has become an increasingly practical tool in modern medicine and agriculture. Among the various gene-editing technologies, CRISPR has attracted the most attention due to its precision, affordability, and relative ease of use compared to earlier methods. CRISPR works like a pair of molecular scissors, allowing scientists to cut DNA at a specific location and either remove, add, or alter genetic material. This technology was originally discovered as part of a natural defense system that bacteria use to fight off viruses. Researchers later realized that the same system could be adapted to edit the genomes of plants, animals, and even humans. In agriculture, gene editing has been used to develop crops that are more resistant to disease, drought, and pests, potentially reducing the need for chemical pesticides. In medicine, scientists are exploring CRISPR's potential to treat genetic disorders by correcting the faulty genes responsible for conditions such as sickle cell anemia. Despite its promise, gene editing raises serious ethical questions. Editing the genes of human embryos, for instance, could permanently alter traits that are passed down to future generations, leading to concerns about unintended consequences and the possibility of creating genetic inequality between those who can afford such treatments and those who cannot. In 2018, a scientist's announcement that he had created the world's first gene-edited babies sparked international outrage and led to calls for stricter regulation. Many countries have since established guidelines limiting the use of gene editing in human reproduction while allowing continued research into its medical applications. As the technology continues to advance, society faces a difficult balancing act: harnessing the benefits of gene editing while carefully managing its risks and ethical implications.
