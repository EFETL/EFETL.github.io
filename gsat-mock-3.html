第一部分　詞彙題（第1-10題）

1.The journalist was praised for her ______ reporting on the civil war, which brought international attention to the crisis.

(A) biased
(B) courageous
(C) fictional
(D) delayed
中譯：這位記者因她對內戰的＿＿＿報導而受到讚揚，這篇報導引起了國際對這場危機的關注。 正解 (B) courageous（勇敢的）：courageous reporting on the civil war（對內戰勇敢的報導）能合理解釋為何記者「受到讚揚」(was praised)，戰地報導往往伴隨危險，記者願意深入報導需要極大的勇氣，與後文「引起國際關注」這一正向結果相呼應。 (A) biased（有偏見的）：biased reporting（有偏見的報導）通常帶有負面意涵，暗示報導不公正、不客觀，這樣的報導不太可能因此「受到讚揚」並引起「國際關注」這種正向評價。 (C) fictional（虛構的）：fictional reporting（虛構的報導）表示報導內容是捏造、不真實的，這與「新聞報導」應具備的真實性背道而馳，且虛構的報導也不會「引起國際對危機的關注」這種具實質意義的反應。 (D) delayed（延遲的）：delayed reporting（延遲的報導）僅描述報導時間上的延誤，與「受到讚揚並引起國際關注」這種強調報導「品質與影響力」的正面評價無關，語意不符。

2.The fashion designer's new collection ______ traditional patterns with modern styles to create a unique look.

(A) separated
(B) eliminated
(C) blended
(D) imitated
中譯：這位時尚設計師的新作品系列將傳統圖案與現代風格＿＿＿，創造出獨特的外觀。 正解 (C) blended（混合、融合）：blended traditional patterns with modern styles（將傳統圖案與現代風格融合）語意通順，"blend...with..." 是常見搭配，表示將兩種不同元素「融合」在一起，產生「unique look（獨特外觀）」這一結果，因果關係合理。 (A) separated（分開）：separated traditional patterns with modern styles（將傳統圖案與現代風格分開）與「創造出獨特外觀」這一強調「融合創新」的結果不符，「分開」兩種元素通常不會產生「融合創新」的效果。 (B) eliminated（消除）：eliminated traditional patterns with modern styles（用現代風格消除傳統圖案）語意不通，若「消除」了傳統圖案，便無法稱為「將傳統圖案與現代風格融合」，且與「創造獨特外觀」（暗示兩種元素並存、融合）的語境矛盾。 (D) imitated（模仿）：imitated traditional patterns with modern styles 語意搭配不自然，"imitate...with..." 不是常見搭配，且「模仿」傳統圖案並不必然產生與「現代風格」融合的「獨特外觀」，blended 更精確傳達「兩種元素結合創新」的概念。

3.The coastal town is vulnerable to flooding, and local authorities are taking steps to ______ the risk.

(A) increase
(B) mitigate
(C) ignore
(D) measure
中譯：這座沿海城鎮容易遭受洪水侵襲，當地政府正在採取措施以＿＿＿這項風險。 正解 (B) mitigate（減輕、緩和）：mitigate the risk（減輕風險）是常見且自然的搭配，"taking steps to mitigate the risk"（採取措施以減輕風險）語意通順，沿海城鎮容易遭受洪水侵襲，政府採取行動的合理目的就是「降低、減輕」這項風險帶來的衝擊。 (A) increase（增加）：increase the risk（增加風險）與「政府採取措施」這一積極應對行動的目的完全相反，政府不會主動採取措施來「增加」洪水風險。 (C) ignore（忽視）：ignore the risk（忽視風險）與「政府正在採取措施」(taking steps) 這一主動、積極的行為描述相互矛盾——如果政府「忽視」風險，就不會「採取措施」。 (D) measure（測量）：measure the risk（測量風險）雖然在邏輯上可能是採取行動前的步驟，但句子強調的是政府「採取措施」這一具體的應對行動，而非單純的「測量、評估」，mitigate（減輕）更直接對應「應對洪水風險」這一具體目的。

4.The historian spent years studying ancient ______ to understand the daily lives of people in the Roman Empire.

(A) forecasts
(B) manuscripts
(C) inventions
(D) broadcasts
中譯：這位歷史學家花費數年研究古代＿＿＿，以了解羅馬帝國時期人們的日常生活。 正解 (B) manuscripts（手稿、文獻）：studying ancient manuscripts to understand the daily lives of people in the Roman Empire（研究古代手稿以了解羅馬帝國時期人們的日常生活）語意通順，歷史學家研究「古代手稿」（如書信、文件等歷史文獻）正是了解過去人們生活方式的重要途徑，邏輯合理。 (A) forecasts（預測）：ancient forecasts（古代的預測）語意不通，「預測」通常指對「未來」的推測，與「研究古代...以了解過去人們的日常生活」這一回顧過去的研究目的方向相反。 (C) inventions（發明）：studying ancient inventions（研究古代發明）雖然語意上可能部分相關（發明也能反映當時生活），但相較於 manuscripts（手稿/文獻，直接記錄了人們的思想與生活細節），inventions（發明，多指物品本身）較難全面說明「人們的日常生活」這一較廣泛的主題。 (D) broadcasts（廣播）：ancient broadcasts（古代的廣播）語意不通，「廣播」是現代科技產物，羅馬帝國時期並不存在「廣播」這種媒介，與「古代」(ancient) 這一時代背景矛盾。

5.The new law is designed to ______ companies from dumping toxic chemicals into local rivers and lakes.

(A) encourage
(B) persuade
(C) prevent
(D) remind
中譯：這項新法律旨在＿＿＿企業將有毒化學物質排放到當地的河流與湖泊中。 正解 (C) prevent（防止、阻止）：prevent companies from dumping toxic chemicals into local rivers and lakes（防止企業將有毒化學物質排放到當地河流與湖泊）是固定搭配 "prevent...from doing..."，語意通順，「新法律」的目的正是要「阻止」企業做出這種污染環境的行為，與「設計這項法律」(designed to) 的立法目的完全吻合。 (A) encourage（鼓勵）：encourage companies from dumping（鼓勵企業從排放）文法搭配不通（"encourage" 通常接 "to do" 而非 "from doing"），且語意上「鼓勵企業排放有毒化學物質」與法律保護環境的立法目的完全相反。 (B) persuade（說服）：persuade companies from dumping 文法搭配不自然（"persuade" 通常接 "not to do" 或 "to do"，而非 "from doing"），且「說服」的強制力遠不如法律「prevent（防止）」所具有的約束力，與「新法律」(new law) 這一具強制性規範的語境不完全吻合。 (D) remind（提醒）：remind companies from dumping 文法搭配不通（"remind" 通常接 "to do" 或 "that..."，而非 "from doing"），且「提醒」企業這種較為被動、溫和的行為，與「新法律」這種具強制約束力的規範性質不符。

6.The CEO's bold decision to ______ in new technology paid off, as profits rose by 40% within a year.

(A) invest
(B) interfere
(C) withdraw
(D) comply
中譯：這位執行長大膽決定＿＿＿新技術，這個決定獲得了回報，因為利潤在一年內成長了40%。 正解 (A) invest（投資）：bold decision to invest in new technology paid off（大膽投資新技術的決定獲得了回報）語意通順，"invest in"（投資於）是固定搭配，企業投資新技術後「利潤成長40%」正是「投資決策獲得回報」(paid off) 的具體成果，因果關係合理。 (B) interfere（干涉）：decision to interfere in new technology（決定干涉新技術）語意不通且搭配不自然，"interfere in" 通常指「干涉某事務」，與「企業透過某種行動使利潤成長40%」的正向商業決策語境不符。 (C) withdraw（撤回、撤資）：decision to withdraw in new technology（決定從新技術中撤資）語意上，如果是「撤資」，通常意味著放棄該領域的投入，這與後文「利潤成長40%」這一正向成果之間的因果關係較難直接建立（撤資較難直接帶來利潤大幅成長）。 (D) comply（遵守、順從）：decision to comply in new technology 文法搭配不通，"comply with" 才是正確搭配（遵守某規定），且「遵守新技術」語意不通，企業「遵守」並非「大膽決定」(bold decision) 所形容的行為類型。

7.Despite working long hours, the nurse remained cheerful and ______ toward the patients in her care.

(A) indifferent
(B) compassionate
(C) impatient
(D) formal
中譯：儘管長時間工作，這位護士對她所照顧的病人仍保持愉快且＿＿＿的態度。 正解 (B) compassionate（有同情心的）：remained cheerful and compassionate toward the patients（對病人保持愉快且富有同情心）語意通順，"compassionate toward"（對...有同情心）是常見搭配，護士對病人展現「同情心」是一種正向、溫暖的特質，與 cheerful（愉快的）並列，皆描述護士正向的工作態度，與「despite working long hours」（儘管長時間工作）形成「儘管辛苦仍保持正向態度」的合理轉折。 (A) indifferent（冷漠的、不在乎的）：cheerful and indifferent（愉快且冷漠）語意矛盾，"indifferent toward the patients"（對病人冷漠）與護士應具備的專業關懷態度相反，且與 cheerful 並列在語意上不協調（一個正向、一個負向）。 (C) impatient（不耐煩的）：cheerful and impatient（愉快且不耐煩）同樣語意矛盾，"impatient toward the patients"（對病人不耐煩）是負面特質，與 cheerful（愉快的）這一正向描述不協調，且與「儘管長時間工作仍保持正向態度」的轉折邏輯不符。 (D) formal（正式的、拘謹的）：cheerful and formal（愉快且正式/拘謹）雖然兩者都非負面詞彙，但 "formal toward the patients"（對病人拘謹、正式）與「despite working long hours」所暗示「儘管辛苦仍展現溫暖關懷」的語境相比，compassionate（富有同情心）更能呼應護士對病人「關懷照顧」的職業精神。

8.The professor argued that the study's findings were ______ because the sample size was too small.

(A) conclusive
(B) controversial
(C) questionable
(D) significant
中譯：這位教授認為這項研究的發現是＿＿＿的，因為樣本量太小。 正解 (C) questionable（值得懷疑的、有疑問的）：findings were questionable because the sample size was too small（研究發現因樣本量太小而值得懷疑）語意通順，"because"（因為）引出的原因「樣本量太小」正是讓研究結果的可信度受到「質疑」的合理原因，因果關係吻合。 (A) conclusive（決定性的、確定的）：findings were conclusive because the sample size was too small（研究發現因樣本量太小而是決定性的）語意矛盾，「樣本量太小」通常會「降低」研究結果的可信度，使其「不具決定性」，而非使其「更具決定性」(conclusive)。 (B) controversial（有爭議的）：findings were controversial because the sample size was too small（研究發現因樣本量太小而具爭議性）雖然語意上可能部分相關，但 "controversial"（有爭議的）通常指因「觀點分歧、立場對立」而引發爭論，而「樣本量太小」這一方法論上的缺陷，更直接導致研究結果的「可信度被質疑」(questionable)，而非「引發爭議」。 (D) significant（顯著的、重要的）：findings were significant because the sample size was too small（研究發現因樣本量太小而是顯著的）語意矛盾，「樣本量太小」通常是研究的「缺陷」，這個缺陷不會使研究結果變得「顯著、重要」，反而會降低其可信度。

9.The young entrepreneur's startup idea was so ______ that investors immediately agreed to fund it.

(A) conventional
(B) risky
(C) promising
(D) complicated
中譯：這位年輕創業家的創業點子如此＿＿＿，以至於投資人立即同意資助它。 正解 (C) promising（有前景的、有潛力的）：startup idea was so promising that investors immediately agreed to fund it（創業點子如此有前景，以至於投資人立即同意資助）語意通順，"so...that..."（如此...以至於...）句型中，"promising"（有前景的）能合理解釋為何「投資人立即同意資助」——點子展現出極佳的發展潛力，投資人因此願意快速投入資金，因果關係合理。 (A) conventional（傳統的、傳統的）：startup idea was so conventional that investors immediately agreed to fund it（創業點子如此傳統，以至於投資人立即同意資助）語意不通，「傳統」(conventional) 通常缺乏新意或競爭優勢，不太可能讓投資人「立即」決定資助，與「立即同意資助」這種積極反應的因果邏輯不符。 (B) risky（風險高的）：startup idea was so risky that investors immediately agreed to fund it（創業點子風險如此高，以至於投資人立即同意資助）語意矛盾，一般而言「風險高」會讓投資人「猶豫、謹慎」，而非「立即同意資助」，因果關係不合理。 (D) complicated（複雜的）：startup idea was so complicated that investors immediately agreed to fund it（創業點子如此複雜，以至於投資人立即同意資助）語意不通，「複雜」本身並非吸引投資的正向因素，且「複雜」與「投資人立即同意資助」之間缺乏合理的因果連結。

10.The team leader asked each member to ______ to the group discussion by sharing their unique insights.

(A) listen
(B) contribute
(C) withdraw
(D) react
中譯：團隊領導要求每位成員透過分享他們獨特的見解來＿＿＿這場小組討論。 正解 (B) contribute（貢獻、貢獻己力）：contribute to the group discussion by sharing their unique insights（透過分享獨特見解為小組討論做出貢獻）是固定搭配 "contribute to"，語意通順，"by sharing their unique insights"（透過分享獨特見解）正是說明每位成員「如何」對討論做出貢獻的具體方式，邏輯合理。 (A) listen（聆聽）：listen to the group discussion by sharing their unique insights（透過分享獨特見解來聆聽小組討論）語意矛盾，"listen"（聆聽）是接收資訊的被動行為，與「by sharing...insights」（透過分享見解，一種主動輸出的行為）邏輯不一致——聆聽不需要透過分享見解來達成。 (C) withdraw（退出、撤回）：withdraw to the group discussion by sharing their unique insights（透過分享獨特見解退出小組討論）語意不通，"withdraw"（退出）與「分享見解」(積極參與) 的行為方向相反，且 "withdraw to" 搭配不自然。 (D) react（反應）：react to the group discussion by sharing their unique insights（透過分享獨特見解對小組討論做出反應）雖然語意上可能部分通順，但 "contribute to"（貢獻於）更精確對應「團隊領導要求每位成員...做出貢獻」這一具體要求的語境，"react"（反應）較為被動且不一定涉及「貢獻」的積極意涵。

第35至38題為題組

The concept of "food miles" refers to the distance food travels from where it is produced to where it is consumed. First coined in the 1990s, the term became a rallying cry for environmentalists who argued that reducing food miles would cut carbon emissions and support local farmers. The logic seemed straightforward: a tomato grown nearby must surely have a smaller environmental footprint than one shipped across the ocean. However, the relationship between food miles and environmental impact turns out to be far more complex. Researchers at Lincoln University in New Zealand found that, in some cases, imported food can actually have a lower carbon footprint than locally grown alternatives. This is because the environmental cost of food is determined not just by transportation but also by the conditions under which it is grown. New Zealand lamb raised on grass in a temperate climate requires far less energy to produce than lamb raised on grain in an energy-intensive indoor facility in Britain, even after accounting for the shipping distance. This finding does not mean that the origin of food is irrelevant to sustainability. Air freight—which is used for highly perishable items like fresh flowers or out-of-season berries—is far more carbon-intensive than sea or land transport and should be minimized where possible. And supporting local farmers does bring benefits beyond the environmental: it maintains agricultural diversity, supports rural economies, and keeps food supply chains short and resilient. The takeaway is that calculating the true environmental cost of food requires looking at the whole picture—from farming method and energy source to packaging and transport mode—rather than focusing on distance alone.

Every morning, millions of people around the world reach for a cup of coffee before starting their day. Coffee is the second most traded commodity in the world, after oil, and is cultivated in over 70 countries across a global "coffee belt" near the equator. Critics argue, however, that fair trade certification can be expensive and bureaucratic, and that the benefits do not always reach the most vulnerable farmers. In recent years, a growing "direct trade" movement has seen specialty coffee roasters bypass the traditional supply chain entirely, building direct relationships with coffee farms and paying prices well above the market rate. While this approach is praised for its transparency and quality focus, it reaches only a tiny fraction of the world's coffee farmers. The challenge of ensuring a fair and sustainable global coffee supply chain remains, as yet, unsolved.

第43至46題為題組

For most of history, the identification of criminal suspects relied heavily on eyewitness testimony. Witnesses were asked to describe a perpetrator, identify them in a lineup, or point them out in court. This method was seen as highly reliable—after all, surely a person who saw a crime with their own eyes could be trusted to identify the criminal. Decades of psychological research have revealed, however, that eyewitness memory is far less reliable than most people assume. Pioneering studies by psychologist Elizabeth Loftus demonstrated that human memory is not like a video recording but is instead reconstructive—shaped and even altered by subsequent experiences and suggestions. In one famous experiment, subjects who watched a film of a car accident were asked either "How fast were the cars going when they hit each other?" or "...when they smashed into each other?" Those who heard the word "smashed" reported significantly higher speed estimates and were more likely to falsely "remember" broken glass that did not appear in the film. The implications of this research for the justice system are profound. Studies of wrongful convictions in the United States have found that mistaken eyewitness identification is the single most common contributing factor, present in more than 70% of cases later overturned through DNA evidence. In response, many countries have reformed their eyewitness identification procedures. Double-blind lineups—where neither the witness nor the lineup administrator knows who the suspect is—have been shown to reduce misidentification. Recording witnesses' statements immediately and warning them that the actual perpetrator may not be present have also been found to improve accuracy. The goal is not to eliminate eyewitness evidence but to collect it in ways that minimize the risk of contamination.
