第一部分　詞彙題（第1-10題）

1.The government announced a series of ______ measures to reduce air pollution in the capital.

(A) aggressive
(B) optional
(C) subtle
(D) decorative
中譯：政府宣布了一系列＿＿＿措施，以減少首都的空氣污染。 正解 (A) aggressive（積極強硬的、大力的）：aggressive measures（積極/強硬的措施）是常見搭配，用來形容政府為解決嚴重問題（如空氣污染）所採取的「大力、積極」的行動，與「減少空氣污染」這一目標的迫切性相符。 (B) optional（可選擇的、非強制的）：optional measures（可選擇性措施）暗示這些措施並非強制執行，與政府「宣布」一系列措施以「解決」污染問題的堅定語氣不符，力度不足。 (C) subtle（微妙的、不明顯的）：subtle measures（微妙的措施）表示不易察覺、輕微的作法，但要有效減少空氣污染通常需要明顯且有力的政策，與句意的迫切性矛盾。 (D) decorative（裝飾性的）：decorative measures（裝飾性措施）指僅具表面、裝飾效果而無實質作用的措施，與政府認真解決污染問題的語境完全不符。

2.The novel's plot was so ______ that even experienced readers found it hard to predict the ending.

(A) predictable
(B) straightforward
(C) intricate
(D) shallow
中譯：這部小說的情節非常＿＿＿，即使是經驗豐富的讀者也很難預測結局。 正解 (C) intricate（複雜精細的）：intricate plot（錯綜複雜的情節）能合理解釋為什麼「即使是經驗豐富的讀者也難以預測結局」，情節越複雜、層次越多，越難猜到走向，因果關係合理。 (A) predictable（可預測的）：若情節是 predictable（可預測的），便不會讓「經驗豐富的讀者也覺得難以預測」，語意自相矛盾。 (B) straightforward（直接了當的、簡單明瞭的）：straightforward plot（簡單直白的情節）通常容易預測結局，與「難以預測」的句意相反。 (D) shallow（膚淺的）：shallow plot（膚淺的情節）通常結構簡單、缺乏深度，較容易被猜到結局，與「難以預測」的語境不符。

3.After the storm, volunteers worked tirelessly to ______ the damaged homes in the flooded neighborhood.

(A) inspect
(B) restore
(C) abandon
(D) measure
中譯：暴風雨過後，志工們不辭辛勞地＿＿＿淹水社區中受損的房屋。 正解 (B) restore（修復、恢復）：restore the damaged homes（修復受損的房屋）語意通順，志工在風災後協助修復房屋是常見且合理的善後行動，與「暴風雨過後」的情境完全吻合。 (A) inspect（檢查）：inspect the damaged homes（檢查受損房屋）雖然也合理，但「不辭辛勞地檢查」與「tirelessly」所強調的「持續努力、付出大量勞力」的語氣相比，restore（修復重建）更能展現志工辛勤付出的具體成果與行動意義。 (C) abandon（放棄、棄置）：abandon the damaged homes（放棄受損房屋）與志工「不辭辛勞」(tirelessly worked) 採取積極行動的精神完全相反，邏輯矛盾。 (D) measure（測量）：measure the damaged homes（測量受損房屋）語意過於技術性、片面，無法呼應「志工們不辭辛勞地努力」這種投入大量心力協助重建的整體語境。

4.The speaker's ______ tone made the audience feel at ease and ready to listen attentively.

(A) hostile
(B) soothing
(C) technical
(D) urgent
中譯：演講者＿＿＿的語氣讓聽眾感到放心並願意專注聆聽。 正解 (B) soothing（撫慰人心的、舒緩的）：soothing tone（撫慰人心的語氣）能讓人感到 at ease（放鬆、安心），並進而「ready to listen attentively」（願意專心聆聽），因果關係合理且語意自然。 (A) hostile（充滿敵意的）：hostile tone（充滿敵意的語氣）會讓聽眾感到緊張或防備，而非「感到放鬆並專心聆聽」，語意相反。 (C) technical（技術性的）：technical tone（技術性的語氣）通常較生硬、專業，不一定能讓聽眾「感到放鬆」，且與「感到放心」的情感描述不直接相關。 (D) urgent（緊迫的、急切的）：urgent tone（急切的語氣）通常會讓人感到緊張、有壓力，而非「感到放鬆、安心」，與句意相反。

5.The athlete's ______ training schedule left little time for family or leisure activities.

(A) flexible
(B) casual
(C) demanding
(D) adequate
中譯：這位運動員＿＿＿的訓練計畫幾乎沒有留下時間給家庭或休閒活動。 正解 (C) demanding（高要求的、嚴苛的）：demanding training schedule（要求嚴苛的訓練計畫）能合理解釋為什麼「幾乎沒有時間給家庭或休閒活動」——訓練計畫越嚴苛、要求越高，所佔用的時間就越多，因果關係合理。 (A) flexible（彈性的）：flexible schedule（彈性的計畫）通常意味著時間安排可以調整，理論上應該「留有時間」給其他活動，與句意「幾乎沒有時間」相矛盾。 (B) casual（隨意的、非正式的）：casual training schedule（隨意的訓練計畫）暗示訓練強度不高、不嚴格，不太可能導致「幾乎沒有時間」從事其他活動，與句意不符。 (D) adequate（足夠的、適當的）：adequate schedule（足夠/適當的計畫）通常表示安排得當、合理，不會造成「幾乎沒有時間」這種極端的結果，與句意的強烈語氣不符。

6.The museum's latest exhibition ______ rare artifacts from civilizations that no longer exist.

(A) features
(B) rejects
(C) removes
(D) donates
中譯：這間博物館最新的展覽＿＿＿來自已不存在文明的稀有文物。 正解 (A) features（以…為特色、展出）：feature rare artifacts（以稀有文物為特色展出）是描述展覽內容時常見且自然的搭配，意指這次展覽「展出、呈現」這些珍貴文物，語意通順。 (B) rejects（拒絕）：reject rare artifacts（拒絕稀有文物）語意不通，展覽的目的是「展示」文物而非「拒絕」它們，邏輯矛盾。 (C) removes（移除）：remove rare artifacts（移除稀有文物）表示將文物撤除、移走，這與「最新展覽」呈現文物的語境完全相反。 (D) donates（捐贈）：donate rare artifacts（捐贈稀有文物）通常指博物館將文物贈與他處，而非「展覽展出」文物，動詞主體與語境不符。

7.To ______ the safety of workers, the factory installed new protective gear and updated equipment.

(A) threaten
(B) determine
(C) guarantee
(D) postpone
中譯：為了＿＿＿工人的安全，工廠安裝了新的防護裝備並更新了設備。 正解 (C) guarantee（保證、確保）：guarantee the safety of workers（保證工人的安全）語意通順，「安裝新的防護裝備並更新設備」正是為了「確保」工人安全所採取的具體行動，因果關係合理。 (A) threaten（威脅）：threaten the safety of workers（威脅工人的安全）與「安裝防護裝備、更新設備」這種保護性行動的目的完全相反，語意矛盾。 (B) determine（決定、判定）：determine the safety of workers（判定工人的安全）語意較為抽象、偏向「評估」而非「確保」，與後文「安裝防護裝備」這種主動採取保護措施的具體行動銜接不夠直接。 (D) postpone（延後）：postpone the safety of workers（延後工人的安全）語意不通，「安全」並非可以被「延後」的對象，且與「安裝新設備」這種立即採取行動的描述矛盾。

8.The report revealed that food waste has reached ______ levels, with millions of tons discarded each year.

(A) manageable
(B) alarming
(C) beneficial
(D) satisfying
中譯：報告顯示食物浪費已達到＿＿＿的程度，每年有數百萬噸的食物被丟棄。 正解 (B) alarming（令人擔憂的、警訊的）：alarming levels（令人擔憂的程度）能合理修飾「食物浪費」這一負面現象，且後文「每年數百萬噸食物被丟棄」這個驚人的數據，正好支持「令人擔憂」的語氣，因果關係合理。 (A) manageable（可控制的、可處理的）：manageable levels（可控制的程度）暗示問題不算嚴重、仍在掌控之中，但後文「每年數百萬噸食物被丟棄」描述的是嚴重的問題，與 manageable 的語意矛盾。 (C) beneficial（有益的）：beneficial levels（有益的程度）用於形容「食物浪費」這種負面現象顯然不合邏輯，浪費食物不可能是「有益的」。 (D) satisfying（令人滿意的）：satisfying levels（令人滿意的程度）同樣不適合用來形容「食物浪費」這個負面議題，語意矛盾且不合常理。

9.His consistent ______ to the team's success was finally acknowledged at the annual awards ceremony.

(A) objection
(B) interference
(C) contribution
(D) reduction
中譯：他對團隊成功持續的＿＿＿終於在年度頒獎典禮上獲得了認可。 正解 (C) contribution（貢獻）：consistent contribution to the team's success（對團隊成功持續的貢獻）語意通順，「貢獻」是會在頒獎典禮上被表揚、認可的正向行為，與「被認可」(acknowledged) 的結果相呼應。 (A) objection（反對、異議）：objection to the team's success（對團隊成功的反對）語意不通，「持續反對團隊的成功」不太可能在頒獎典禮上獲得「認可」與表揚，邏輯矛盾。 (B) interference（干涉、妨礙）：interference to the team's success（對團隊成功的干涉/妨礙）是負面行為，不會在頒獎典禮上被「acknowledged」（讚揚、認可），與句意完全相反。 (D) reduction（減少）：reduction to the team's success（對團隊成功的減少）語意不通且搭配不自然，「減少成功」是負面的，不會是頒獎典禮表揚的對象。

10.The landscape was breathtaking—rolling hills ______ by dense forests and sparkling rivers.

(A) damaged
(B) surrounded
(C) divided
(D) covered
中譯：景色令人驚嘆——連綿起伏的丘陵被茂密的森林與閃閃發光的河流＿＿＿。 正解 (B) surrounded（環繞）：rolling hills surrounded by dense forests and sparkling rivers（被茂密森林與閃閃發光的河流環繞的連綿丘陵）描繪出一幅自然景觀互相環繞、層次豐富的畫面，與「breathtaking（令人驚嘆的）landscape」的描述相符，語意自然優美。 (A) damaged（損壞）：damaged by dense forests and sparkling rivers（被茂密森林和閃閃發光的河流「損壞」）語意不通，森林與河流通常不會「損壞」丘陵，且與「breathtaking」讚嘆美景的正面語氣矛盾。 (C) divided（分割）：divided by dense forests and sparkling rivers（被森林和河流「分割」）雖然在文法上可能通順，但「分割」這個詞較為中性甚至帶有割裂感，不如 surrounded（環繞）更能呈現「令人驚嘆」景觀中自然元素彼此交融環繞的和諧美感。 (D) covered（覆蓋）：covered by dense forests and sparkling rivers（被森林和河流「覆蓋」）語意較不自然，河流通常無法「覆蓋」丘陵（河流是線狀分布而非覆蓋整片區域），搭配不夠精確。

第35至38題為題組

For most of human history, sleep has been treated as little more than a necessary inconvenience—time stolen from productive activity. In the 19th century, the inventor Thomas Edison famously declared sleep a waste of time and bragged that he could function on just a few hours per night. Today, scientists are uncovering the full extent of what happens in the brain and body during sleep, and the findings are extraordinary. During sleep, the brain does not simply power down. On the contrary, it becomes extremely active in ways that are essential for health. One of the most important functions of sleep is memory consolidation. During the deep stages of sleep, the hippocampus—a brain region crucial for forming new memories—replays events from the day and transfers important information into long-term storage. This is why sleep deprivation so dramatically impairs learning and recall. Sleep also plays a critical role in physical health. Growth hormone, which repairs tissues and builds muscle, is primarily released during deep sleep. The immune system, too, is reinforced during sleep, as the body produces proteins called cytokines that fight infection and inflammation. Chronic sleep deprivation has been linked to a significantly higher risk of obesity, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and even certain cancers. Despite this knowledge, modern society remains deeply sleep-deprived. Artificial lighting, smartphones, demanding work schedules, and social norms that equate busyness with success all conspire to keep people awake. Public health experts are calling for a cultural shift—one that treats sleep not as laziness, but as a fundamental pillar of well-being.

第39至42題為題組

Originating in Japan, the art of kintsugi—which translates as "golden joinery" or "golden repair"—is a centuries-old practice of mending broken pottery with lacquer mixed with powdered gold, silver, or platinum. Rather than hiding the damage, kintsugi highlights the fractures, treating them as a beautiful and integral part of the object's history. The result is a repaired piece that is arguably more beautiful and more valuable than the original. The philosophy behind kintsugi is deeply connected to the Japanese concept of wabi-sabi, which finds beauty in imperfection and transience. Objects that show signs of age, wear, and repair are considered more aesthetically interesting than those that are flawless. In a broader sense, kintsugi reflects a cultural attitude toward hardship: rather than pretending that difficult experiences never happened, one should acknowledge them and allow them to become part of one's identity. In recent years, kintsugi has attracted considerable attention in the West, not only as an art form but as a psychological metaphor. Therapists and counselors have adopted the concept to discuss resilience, recovery, and the acceptance of personal history. Just as a broken bowl can be made whole and beautiful through golden repair, human beings can emerge from trauma or failure stronger and more interesting than before. Workshops teaching kintsugi have sprung up in cities around the world, drawing participants who are as interested in the philosophy as the craft. For many, the act of carefully mending something broken serves as a meditative practice—a reminder that imperfection is not the end of beauty, but often the beginning of a deeper kind.
