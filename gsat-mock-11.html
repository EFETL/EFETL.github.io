第一部分　詞彙題（第1-10題）

1.The new policy was met with ______ from employees who feared it would reduce their benefits.

(A) admiration
(B) resistance
(C) curiosity
(D) gratitude
中譯：這項新政策遭到員工的＿＿＿，他們擔心這會減少自己的福利。 正解 (B) resistance（抵抗、反對）：be met with resistance（遭到反對/抵制）是常見搭配，員工因「擔心福利減少」而對新政策產生抗拒心理，resistance 完全符合「反對」之意。 (A) admiration（欽佩）：員工擔心福利減少，不太可能對政策感到欽佩，語意與「fear」造成的負面情緒相反。 (C) curiosity（好奇）：好奇是中性、探索性的情緒，與「fear（擔心）」所引發的「抗拒」反應不符。 (D) gratitude（感激）：員工因擔心利益受損而產生的應是負面反應，不會是「感激」，語意矛盾。

2.The museum's latest exhibit ______ the daily lives of ancient Egyptian farmers through interactive displays.

(A) ignores
(B) reconstructs
(C) cancels
(D) confuses
中譯：這座博物館最新的展覽透過互動式展示＿＿＿了古埃及農民的日常生活。 正解 (B) reconstructs（重建、重現）：reconstruct the daily lives（重現日常生活）是博物館展覽常見的描述方式，透過互動展示「重現」古代生活場景，與句意完全吻合。 (A) ignores（忽視）：展覽的目的是呈現內容，而非「忽視」其主題，語意矛盾。 (C) cancels（取消）：cancels the daily lives 語意不通，「取消日常生活」邏輯上講不通。 (D) confuses（使困惑）：展覽透過互動展示應是「幫助理解」而非「使人困惑」，與正向描述展覽效果的語境不符。

3.Although the hikers were exhausted, they pressed on with ______ determination to reach the summit before dark.

(A) fragile
(B) unwavering
(C) occasional
(D) doubtful
中譯：儘管登山者已經疲憊不堪，他們仍以＿＿＿的決心繼續前進，要在天黑前到達山頂。 正解 (B) unwavering（不動搖的、堅定不移的）：unwavering determination（堅定不移的決心）與前面 Although the hikers were exhausted, they pressed on（儘管疲憊仍繼續前進）形成合理呼應，展現出儘管疲勞仍堅持不懈的精神。 (A) fragile（脆弱的）：fragile determination（脆弱的決心）與「pressed on」（繼續前進、堅持）的堅毅語氣矛盾。 (C) occasional（偶爾的）：occasional determination 語意不通，「偶爾的決心」無法支撐「繼續前進直到天黑前登頂」這樣持續的行動。 (D) doubtful（懷疑的、不確定的）：doubtful determination 自相矛盾，「懷疑的決心」邏輯不通，且與「pressed on」展現的堅定態度相反。

4.The company's profits ______ sharply after it introduced a popular new product line.

(A) declined
(B) vanished
(C) soared
(D) stalled
中譯：這家公司推出一款受歡迎的新產品線後，利潤＿＿＿上升。 正解 (C) soared（飆升、急遽上升）：soared sharply（急遽飆升）語意通順，「推出受歡迎的新產品線」導致利潤大幅成長，soared 強調快速且大幅的上升，與 sharply 搭配自然。 (A) declined（下降）：declined sharply（急遽下降）與「推出受歡迎新產品」這個正向原因造成的結果方向相反。 (B) vanished（消失）：vanished sharply 語法搭配不通，vanish 通常不與 sharply 連用，且「利潤消失」與「推出受歡迎產品」邏輯不符。 (D) stalled（停滯）：stalled sharply 搭配不自然，stalled 表示「停滯不前」，與「sharply」所暗示的劇烈變化方向矛盾，也與正面結果不符。

5.Volunteers worked together to ______ the flooded village, clearing debris and rebuilding homes.

(A) restore
(B) abandon
(C) postpone
(D) duplicate
中譯：志工們齊心協力＿＿＿這座遭洪水侵襲的村莊，清除瓦礫並重建房屋。 正解 (A) restore（修復、重建）：restore the flooded village（修復受洪水侵襲的村莊），與後面「clearing debris and rebuilding homes」（清除瓦礫並重建房屋）的具體行動完全呼應，restore 表達「使恢復原狀」之意。 (B) abandon（放棄）：abandon the flooded village（放棄這座村莊）與後文「清除瓦礫、重建房屋」這些積極協助的行動完全相反。 (C) postpone（延後）：postpone the flooded village 語法及語意均不通，postpone 通常接事件或計畫，不能接「村莊」這個地點受詞。 (D) duplicate（複製）：duplicate the flooded village（複製這座村莊）語意不通，與志工們協助重建的目的無關。

6.The professor's lecture was so ______ that several students fell asleep within minutes.

(A) thrilling
(B) tedious
(C) controversial
(D) concise
中譯：這位教授的課過於＿＿＿，導致幾名學生在幾分鐘內就睡著了。 正解 (B) tedious（冗長乏味的）：tedious lecture（乏味的講座）能合理解釋「學生在幾分鐘內睡著」這一結果，tedious 強調無趣、令人厭煩的特性，與「so...that」的因果句型搭配自然。 (A) thrilling（令人興奮的）：thrilling lecture（令人興奮的講座）應該會讓學生保持專注而非睡著，與結果矛盾。 (C) controversial（有爭議的）：有爭議的講座通常會引發討論或情緒反應，而非讓學生「睡著」，因果關係不合理。 (D) concise（簡潔的）：concise lecture（簡潔的講座）通常代表精簡有效，不太可能導致學生在幾分鐘內就睡著，與「so...that」強烈的負面結果不搭。

7.Scientists were ______ to discover that the newly identified species could survive in nearly boiling water.

(A) astonished
(B) indifferent
(C) annoyed
(D) ashamed
中譯：科學家們對於發現這個新確認的物種能在接近沸騰的水中存活感到＿＿＿。 正解 (A) astonished（驚訝的、震驚的）：be astonished to discover（驚訝地發現）符合「發現物種能在接近沸騰的水中存活」這個出乎常理、令人震驚的事實，astonished 表達強烈的驚奇情緒。 (B) indifferent（無動於衷的）：科學家面對如此罕見且驚人的發現，不太可能「無動於衷」，與情境不符。 (C) annoyed（惱怒的）：發現新物種的驚人能力應是令人興奮或驚訝的事，而非令人「惱怒」，情緒方向不符。 (D) ashamed（感到羞愧的）：「發現新物種的特殊能力」與「羞愧」毫無邏輯關聯，情緒不符語境。

8.The negotiations between the two companies eventually reached a ______ that satisfied both sides.

(A) collapse
(B) compromise
(C) dispute
(D) shortage
中譯：兩家公司之間的談判最終達成了一項讓雙方都滿意的＿＿＿。 正解 (B) compromise（妥協、折衷方案）：reached a compromise that satisfied both sides（達成一項讓雙方都滿意的妥協方案）是談判常見的結果，compromise 強調雙方各退一步、達成共識，與「satisfied both sides」完全吻合。 (A) collapse（破裂、崩潰）：collapse that satisfied both sides 語意矛盾，「談判破裂」不可能讓雙方都滿意。 (C) dispute（爭議、糾紛）：reached a dispute 語法不通，dispute 是談判「過程中」的衝突，而非談判「達成」的結果。 (D) shortage（短缺）：shortage that satisfied both sides 語意不通，「短缺」與「讓雙方滿意的談判結果」毫無關聯。

9.The city council voted to ______ the old factory and turn the site into a public park.

(A) demolish
(B) inherit
(C) imitate
(D) overestimate
中譯：市議會投票決定＿＿＿這座老舊工廠，並將該地改建為公共公園。 正解 (A) demolish（拆除）：demolish the old factory and turn the site into a public park（拆除老舊工廠並將該地改建為公園），demolish（拆除）是將舊建築移除以便進行後續改建的合理動作，與後文邏輯一致。 (B) inherit（繼承）：inherit the old factory（繼承這座工廠）與「將該地改建為公園」的行動邏輯無關，繼承不涉及拆除或改建的決定。 (C) imitate（模仿）：imitate the old factory（模仿這座工廠）語意不通，「模仿一座工廠」無法合理導向「改建為公園」的結果。 (D) overestimate（高估）：overestimate the old factory（高估這座工廠）語意不通且與「改建為公園」的具體行動無關。

10.Her ______ approach to problem-solving allowed her to consider several solutions before choosing one.

(A) careless
(B) systematic
(C) impulsive
(D) outdated
中譯：她解決問題的方式很＿＿＿，讓她在選定方案之前能考慮多種解決辦法。 正解 (B) systematic（系統性的、有條理的）：systematic approach（系統性的方法）能合理解釋「在選擇之前考慮多種解決方案」(consider several solutions before choosing one) 這種有條理、按部就班的思考過程，systematic 強調按部就班、有組織的特性。 (A) careless（粗心的）：careless approach（粗心的方法）與「考慮多種解決方案後再選擇」這種審慎的行為完全相反。 (C) impulsive（衝動的）：impulsive approach（衝動的方法）意指未經思考便行動，與「考慮多種方案後再選擇」這種深思熟慮的態度矛盾。 (D) outdated（過時的）：outdated approach（過時的方法）與「考慮多種解決方案」這種積極、有效的問題解決方式沒有直接關聯，且帶有負面意味，與句子讚揚此approach的語氣不符。

For most of human history, the deep ocean was almost entirely unexplored, hidden beneath layers of darkness and crushing pressure. Even today, scientists estimate that more than 80 percent of the ocean remains unmapped and unexplored, making it one of the last true frontiers on our planet. In fact, we have better maps of the surface of Mars than we do of the ocean floor. Exploring these depths requires highly specialized equipment. Submersibles and remotely operated vehicles, or ROVs, are built to withstand pressures that would instantly crush an unprotected human body. These vehicles are equipped with bright lights, since sunlight cannot penetrate beyond about 1,000 meters, and the rest of the ocean exists in permanent darkness. Cameras and sensors attached to ROVs allow researchers on the surface to observe creatures and geological formations in real time, often discovering species that have never been documented before. Among the most surprising discoveries are hydrothermal vents, cracks in the ocean floor where mineral-rich water heated by volcanic activity spews into the cold ocean. Around these vents, entire ecosystems thrive without any sunlight at all. Instead of relying on plants, these communities depend on bacteria that convert chemicals from the vents into energy through a process called chemosynthesis. Giant tube worms, ghostly white crabs, and other strange creatures cluster around these vents, forming food webs unlike anything found near the surface. Beyond scientific curiosity, deep sea exploration has practical importance. Researchers hope that organisms living in extreme conditions may hold clues for new medicines, since their unusual biology has evolved to cope with intense pressure, heat, and chemical environments. At the same time, growing interest in deep sea mining for valuable minerals has raised concerns among scientists, who warn that disturbing these fragile ecosystems before we even understand them could cause irreversible damage.

Music has long been known to stir emotions, but recent advances in neuroscience have revealed just how deeply it affects the brain. When we listen to a song we love, brain scans show activity in regions associated with reward and pleasure, the same areas that respond to food, money, and other things we find satisfying. This may explain why a favorite song can instantly improve someone's mood or bring back vivid memories from years ago. One particularly striking area of research involves patients with memory disorders such as Alzheimer's disease. Even when patients can no longer recognize close family members or recall recent events, many can still sing along to songs from their youth, sometimes with surprising accuracy. Researchers believe this is because musical memory is stored differently in the brain than other types of memory, in regions that tend to remain relatively undamaged even as the disease progresses. This discovery has led to the growing use of music therapy in nursing homes and hospitals, where familiar songs can calm agitation, reduce anxiety, and occasionally allow patients brief moments of connection with loved ones. Music also appears to influence physical performance. Studies have shown that athletes who listen to upbeat music during exercise often report less perceived effort and can sustain activity longer than those who exercise in silence. Tempo seems to matter as well; faster music tends to encourage faster movements, which is why many runners create playlists matched to their target pace. While scientists continue to debate exactly why music has such powerful effects, most agree that its influence on mood, memory, and movement reflects something fundamental about how the human brain is wired—perhaps a result of music's deep roots in human history and communication.

In 1856, workers quarrying limestone in a valley near Düsseldorf, Germany, uncovered a set of strange bones, including a thick skull and curved limb bones. At first, some scientists dismissed the remains as belonging to a deformed modern human or an unusual animal. However, further analysis revealed that the bones belonged to an entirely different type of human, one that had lived in Europe long before modern humans arrived. This discovery gave the species its name: Neanderthals, after the Neander Valley where the bones were found. For more than a century, Neanderthals were often portrayed in popular culture as dim-witted, brutish cavemen, lacking the intelligence and creativity of modern humans. However, decades of additional research have dramatically changed this picture. Archaeologists have found evidence that Neanderthals made sophisticated tools, controlled fire, wore clothing made from animal hides, and even created jewelry from shells and eagle talons. Some sites suggest that Neanderthals buried their dead with care, possibly indicating beliefs about death or an afterlife. Perhaps the most surprising discovery came from genetic research. By analyzing DNA extracted from Neanderthal bones, scientists found that Neanderthals and early modern humans interbred when their paths crossed in Europe and Asia tens of thousands of years ago. As a result, most people of non-African descent today carry small amounts of Neanderthal DNA, inherited from these ancient encounters. Some of these genetic traces have been linked to traits such as skin pigmentation, immune system responses, and even sleep patterns. Neanderthals disappeared roughly 40,000 years ago, and scientists still debate why. Possible explanations include climate change, competition with modern humans for resources, and small population sizes that made the species vulnerable to extinction. Whatever the cause, the Neanderthal story has been transformed from one of primitive failure to one of a resilient and resourceful species that, in a sense, still lives on within us today.
