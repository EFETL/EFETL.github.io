第一部分　詞彙題（第1-10題）

1.The new bridge was designed to ______ heavy traffic during rush hour without causing major delays.

(A) accommodate
(B) eliminate
(C) postpone
(D) disguise
中譯：這座新橋的設計是為了在尖峰時段＿＿＿大量車流，而不會造成嚴重延誤。 正解 (A) accommodate（容納、應付）：accommodate heavy traffic（容納/應付大量車流）語意通順，指橋梁的設計能夠承載並順利疏導大量車輛，與後文「without causing major delays」（不會造成嚴重延誤）的正向結果相呼應。 (B) eliminate（消除）：eliminate heavy traffic（消除大量車流）語意過強且不合理，橋梁的功能是「疏導」車流，並非「消除」交通量本身。 (C) postpone（延後）：postpone heavy traffic（延後車流）語意不通，「車流」並非可以被「延後」的對象，且與橋梁設計的功能無關。 (D) disguise（偽裝、掩飾）：disguise heavy traffic（偽裝車流）語意完全不通，偽裝通常用於隱藏外觀或身份，與橋梁疏導交通的功能毫無關聯。

2.Despite the heavy rain, the outdoor concert went ahead as planned, much to the ______ of the organizers.

(A) relief
(B) disappointment
(C) confusion
(D) embarrassment
中譯：儘管下著大雨，這場戶外音樂會仍按計畫進行，讓主辦單位感到非常＿＿＿。 正解 (A) relief（寬慰、放心）：much to the relief of the organizers（讓主辦單位大感寬慰）是常見搭配，句首 Despite the heavy rain（儘管下大雨）暗示原本擔心活動可能取消或受影響，但音樂會仍「went ahead as planned」（按計畫進行），這個結果讓主辦單位感到「鬆了一口氣、寬慰」，relief 完美呼應這種「擔憂解除後的安心感」。 (B) disappointment（失望）：若音樂會「按計畫順利進行」，主辦單位應感到欣慰而非失望，disappointment 與句子描述的正面結果相矛盾。 (C) confusion（困惑）：confusion 表示對某事感到不解或混亂，但「活動按計畫進行」是一個明確、清楚的結果，並不會讓主辦單位感到困惑。 (D) embarrassment（尷尬）：embarrassment 通常源自令人難堪或丟臉的情況，但音樂會順利進行是值得高興的結果，與「尷尬」的語意方向相反。

3.The professor's theory was initially ______ by other scientists, but later experiments proved her correct.

(A) supported
(B) dismissed
(C) celebrated
(D) confirmed
中譯：這位教授的理論最初被其他科學家＿＿＿，但後來的實驗證明她是對的。 正解 (B) dismissed（駁回、不予理會）：句子後半部用 but（但是）表示轉折——「但後來的實驗證明她是對的」(later experiments proved her correct)，這暗示理論「最初」(initially) 並未被接受、甚至遭到否定，dismissed（被駁回、被認為不值得重視）與後文「被證明是對的」形成「先被否定、後被證實」的合理對比。 (A) supported（被支持）：若理論「最初」就被支持，後面用 but 接「後來證明她是對的」便顯得語意重複、缺乏轉折，與句子的對比邏輯不符。 (C) celebrated（被讚揚）：若理論最初就被讚揚，與後文「但後來證明她是對的」之間沒有合理的轉折關係，celebrated 通常用於描述已被認可、推崇的事物。 (D) confirmed（被證實）：若理論最初就「被證實」，那麼後文「but later experiments proved her correct」便顯得重複且矛盾——理論不可能先被證實，後來才又被證實一次。

4.The company's annual report revealed a ______ increase in profits compared to the previous year.

(A) negligible
(B) substantial
(C) doubtful
(D) imaginary
中譯：該公司的年度報告顯示，與前一年相比，利潤有＿＿＿的增長。 正解 (B) substantial（大量的、顯著的）：a substantial increase in profits（利潤大幅增長）是常見且自然的搭配，substantial 用來形容數量、程度上「相當可觀、顯著」的增加，符合「年度報告中呈現的利潤成長」這種具體、值得report的數據描述。 (A) negligible（微不足道的）：若利潤增長是「微不足道」的，通常不會特別作為年度報告中的重點來呈現，且 negligible increase 語意上較為消極，與報告強調成長的語境不符。 (C) doubtful（令人懷疑的）：doubtful increase（令人懷疑的增長）語意不通，「增長」是一個已發生的客觀事實/數據，不會用「令人懷疑」來形容其本身的程度。 (D) imaginary（虛構的、想像中的）：imaginary increase（虛構的增長）表示這個增長並不真實存在，但年度報告呈現的是「revealed（顯示出）」的實際數據，與「虛構」的語意矛盾。

5.The detective carefully examined the crime scene, looking for any ______ that might reveal what had happened.

(A) clues
(B) rumors
(C) excuses
(D) compliments
中譯：這位探員仔細檢查了犯罪現場，尋找任何可能揭露事發經過的＿＿＿。 正解 (A) clues（線索）：The detective...looking for any clues that might reveal what had happened（探員尋找任何可能揭露事發經過的線索）語意完全通順，clues（線索）正是偵探在犯罪現場仔細搜尋的對象，符合常見的辦案情境描述。 (B) rumors（謠言）：rumors（謠言）通常是人們口頭流傳、未經證實的訊息，並非偵探在「犯罪現場」透過仔細檢查所能找到的實體證據或跡象，與情境不符。 (C) excuses（藉口）：excuses（藉口）是某人為自己的行為辯解的說法，並非偵探在現場「檢查」就能找到的東西，與「揭露事發經過」的目的無關。 (D) compliments（讚美）：compliments（讚美）與犯罪現場調查的情境完全無關，探員不會在犯罪現場尋找「讚美」。

6.The charity relies entirely on ______ donations from individuals and businesses to fund its programs.

(A) mandatory
(B) voluntary
(C) overdue
(D) hidden
中譯：這間慈善機構完全依賴來自個人與企業的＿＿＿捐款來資助其項目。 正解 (B) voluntary（自願的）：voluntary donations（自願捐款）是固定且常見的搭配，慈善機構的捐款本質上是捐贈者「自願」提供的，與「relies entirely on...donations...to fund its programs」（完全依賴...捐款來資助項目）的語境完全吻合。 (A) mandatory（強制性的）：mandatory donations（強制性捐款）在語意上自相矛盾——「捐款」(donation) 本身即帶有自願性質，若是「強制」繳納，性質上更接近稅金或費用，而非donation。 (C) overdue（過期的、逾期未付的）：overdue donations（逾期捐款）通常用於描述應付款項逾期未繳，與慈善機構「依賴」捐款來運作的正向、持續性語境不符。 (D) hidden（隱藏的）：hidden donations（隱藏的捐款）語意不通，慈善機構依賴的捐款應是公開、可被機構運用的資金，而非「隱藏」起來的款項。

7.Many endangered species face the ______ threat of habitat loss due to deforestation.

(A) imaginary
(B) constant
(C) occasional
(D) former
中譯：許多瀕危物種因森林砍伐而面臨棲息地喪失的＿＿＿威脅。 正解 (B) constant（持續不斷的）：the constant threat of habitat loss（持續不斷的棲息地喪失威脅）語意通順，due to deforestation（因森林砍伐）描述的是一個持續發生、未曾停止的過程，因此瀕危物種所面臨的威脅是「持續性」的，constant 完美呼應這種長期、不間斷的威脅性質。 (A) imaginary（虛構的、想像中的）：imaginary threat（虛構的威脅）表示這個威脅並不真實存在，但森林砍伐導致棲息地喪失是真實發生的環境問題，與 imaginary 的語意矛盾。 (C) occasional（偶爾的）：occasional threat（偶爾的威脅）暗示威脅是間歇性、不常發生的，但森林砍伐造成的棲息地喪失是持續累積、長期存在的問題，與 occasional 的語意不符。 (D) former（先前的、以前的）：former threat（先前的威脅）表示這個威脅已經是過去式、不再存在，但句子描述的是物種「目前正面臨」(face) 的威脅，與 former 的時態語意矛盾。

8.The negotiations reached a ______ when both sides refused to make any further concessions.

(A) breakthrough
(B) deadlock
(C) celebration
(D) discount
中譯：當雙方都拒絕做出進一步讓步時，談判陷入了＿＿＿。 正解 (B) deadlock（僵局）：reached a deadlock（陷入僵局）是談判語境中常見的固定搭配，when both sides refused to make any further concessions（當雙方都拒絕做出進一步讓步時）正是描述談判「卡住、無法前進」的狀態，與 deadlock（僵局，雙方互不退讓導致的停滯狀態）完全吻合。 (A) breakthrough（突破）：breakthrough 表示談判取得了重大進展或突破，但句子描述的是「雙方拒絕讓步」這種停滯不前的負面情況，與 breakthrough 的正向語意完全相反。 (C) celebration（慶祝）：celebration（慶祝）通常發生在達成協議或取得成功之後，但「雙方拒絕讓步」顯然不是值得慶祝的結果，語意不符。 (D) discount（折扣）：discount（折扣）是商業交易中價格上的減讓，與「談判陷入...」這種描述談判狀態的語境完全無關，搭配不當。

9.The author's writing style is known for its ______ descriptions, which allow readers to picture every scene vividly.

(A) vivid
(B) dull
(C) careless
(D) brief
中譯：這位作家的寫作風格以其＿＿＿的描寫著稱，讓讀者能夠生動地想像出每一個場景。 正解 (A) vivid（生動的、鮮明的）：vivid descriptions（生動的描寫）與後文「allow readers to picture every scene vividly」（讓讀者能夠生動地想像每個場景）完全呼應——正因為描寫「vivid」（生動鮮明），讀者才能在腦中清晰地「picture」（想像）出畫面，前後語意一致且互相支持。 (B) dull（乏味的、單調的）：dull descriptions（乏味的描寫）與後文「讓讀者能生動地想像每個場景」的效果完全矛盾——乏味的描寫通常難以讓讀者產生生動的畫面感。 (C) careless（粗心的、草率的）：careless descriptions（草率的描寫）通常意味著缺乏細節、品質不佳，難以達到「讓讀者生動地想像每個場景」這種需要細膩描繪才能產生的效果，語意不符。 (D) brief（簡短的）：brief descriptions（簡短的描寫）強調篇幅短小、內容精簡，但「讓讀者能生動地想像每一個場景」通常需要較為詳盡、細膻的描寫，與「簡短」的特性不完全吻合，不如 vivid 直接呼應後文的 vividly。

10.The committee will ______ the proposal carefully before deciding whether to approve the funding.

(A) discard
(B) evaluate
(C) ignore
(D) postpone
中譯：委員會將在決定是否核准這項資金前，仔細＿＿＿這項提案。 正解 (B) evaluate（評估）：will evaluate the proposal carefully before deciding whether to approve the funding（在決定是否核准資金前仔細評估這項提案）語意通順，evaluate（評估、審核）正是委員會在做出核准與否的決定前，所應採取的審慎行動，與「carefully」（仔細地）和「before deciding」（在決定之前）的語境完全吻合。 (A) discard（丟棄、捨棄）：discard the proposal carefully before deciding（在決定前仔細丟棄提案）語意矛盾——若提案已被「丟棄」，便沒有後續「決定是否核准資金」的必要，邏輯不通。 (C) ignore（忽視）：ignore the proposal carefully（仔細地忽視提案）本身用詞矛盾——「忽視」表示不予關注，但「carefully」（仔細地）暗示需要投入注意力，兩者語意衝突；且若委員會「忽視」提案，便無法做出「是否核准資金」的決定。 (D) postpone（延後）：postpone the proposal carefully before deciding whether to approve the funding（在決定是否核准資金前仔細延後這項提案）語意不通且累贅——句子已經說明委員會「將在決定前」採取某行動，若該行動是「延後」，則與「before deciding」（在做決定之前）的時間邏輯重複矛盾。

In the early 1990s, a small group of biologists proposed an unusual experiment: seal a group of people inside a sealed glass and steel structure for two years, growing their own food and recycling their own air and water, with no exchange of matter with the outside world. The project, known as Biosphere 2, was built in the Arizona desert and remains one of the most ambitious attempts to create a closed ecological system. Inside the massive structure, researchers created several miniature ecosystems, including a rainforest, a desert, a small ocean with a coral reef, and agricultural areas for growing crops. Eight "biospherians" lived inside the facility, tasked with maintaining the delicate balance of these interconnected systems while also growing enough food to feed themselves. Despite these difficulties, the biospherians completed the full two-year mission, and the project provided valuable data on the complexity of maintaining closed ecological systems. Researchers gained insights into atmospheric chemistry, the challenges of producing enough food in a limited space, and the delicate balance required to keep an ecosystem stable. While Biosphere 2 did not achieve everything its creators had hoped, it remains an important reference point for scientists studying life support systems for potential future missions to the Moon or Mars, where astronauts would similarly need to grow food and recycle resources in a closed environment.

Throughout history, certain colors have carried meanings far beyond their visual appearance, often shaping politics, religion, and social status. The color purple, for example, was once so rare and expensive to produce that it became closely associated with royalty and power. In ancient times, purple dye was extracted from a type of sea snail found along the Mediterranean coast. Producing even a small amount of dye required thousands of snails, making purple cloth extraordinarily costly. As a result, in many ancient societies, wearing purple was restricted to emperors, kings, and high-ranking officials, while ordinary citizens were forbidden from wearing the color regardless of their wealth. This association between purple and power persisted for centuries, even after the original snail-based dye was lost following the fall of certain empires. The color continued to symbolize wealth and status long after the original economic reasons for its rarity had disappeared. Everything changed in the mid-19th century, when a young chemist accidentally discovered a way to produce a vivid purple dye from coal tar while attempting to create a treatment for a disease. This discovery made purple dye cheap and widely available for the first time in history, allowing people from all social classes to wear the color. In many parts of the world, traditional handicrafts that were once essential to daily life are now at risk of disappearing as younger generations pursue other careers and mass-produced alternatives become widely available. From hand-woven textiles to hand-carved furniture, these crafts often represent centuries of accumulated knowledge passed down from one generation to the next. In response, various organizations and governments have launched initiatives to document and preserve traditional craft techniques before the artisans who practice them retire or pass away. These efforts often involve recording detailed videos of the crafting process, interviewing master artisans about techniques that have never been written down, and in some cases providing financial support to encourage young people to learn these skills. Some communities have found that connecting traditional crafts to tourism can provide both economic incentives and renewed interest. Visitors who purchase handmade goods directly from artisans often pay a premium, and workshops where tourists can try crafting techniques themselves have become popular attractions in some regions. However, balancing preservation with commercial demand presents its own challenges. Critics warn that when traditional crafts become primarily oriented toward tourists, the items produced may be simplified or altered to suit foreign tastes, potentially losing the cultural significance and craftsmanship that made them valuable in the first place. Despite these tensions, many artisans and cultural advocates remain hopeful. Online marketplaces have opened up new opportunities for artisans to sell their work directly to customers around the world, sometimes allowing them to earn a sustainable living from their craft without relying entirely on local markets or tourism. Ultimately, the survival of many traditional crafts may depend on finding a balance between honoring their cultural roots and adapting to the realities of a changing economy.
