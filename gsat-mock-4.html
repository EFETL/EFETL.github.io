第一部分　詞彙題（第1-10題）

1.The new policy was met with widespread ______ from citizens who felt their opinions had been ignored.

(A) admiration
(B) resentment
(C) curiosity
(D) generosity
中譯：這項新政策遭到許多公民的廣泛＿＿＿，他們覺得自己的意見被忽視了。 正解 (B) resentment（憤恨、不滿）：句子說明公民「覺得自己的意見被忽視」(felt their opinions had been ignored)，這種被忽視的感受自然會引發「憤恨、不滿」的情緒，resentment 與 widespread（廣泛的）搭配，描述大眾對政策的不滿反應，語意完全吻合。 (A) admiration（欽佩、讚賞）：admiration 是正面、欣賞的情緒，與「覺得意見被忽視」這種負面感受完全相反，邏輯不符。 (C) curiosity（好奇心）：curiosity 表示對某事物感到好奇、想了解，與「因意見被忽視而產生的不滿情緒」毫無關聯，語意不符。 (D) generosity（慷慨）：generosity 是描述人或行為「大方、慷慨」的特質，與公民對政策的「情緒反應」這個語境完全無關。

2.Engineers had to ______ the bridge design after the initial tests revealed serious safety concerns.

(A) duplicate
(B) abandon
(C) revise
(D) ignore
中譯：在初步測試顯示出嚴重的安全隱憂後，工程師們必須＿＿＿橋樑設計。 正解 (C) revise（修訂、修改）：句子說明「初步測試顯示出嚴重的安全隱憂」(initial tests revealed serious safety concerns)，面對這種問題，最合理的做法是「修改」設計以解決安全隱憂，revise 表示針對既有設計做調整與改善，與句意完全吻合。 (A) duplicate（複製）：複製原本的設計無法解決「安全隱憂」的問題，若設計本身有缺陷，複製只會複製問題，邏輯不通。 (B) abandon（放棄）：若工程師「放棄」整個橋樑設計，意味著專案中止，但句子強調的是針對「safety concerns」採取行動，revise（修改）比 abandon（完全放棄）更符合「解決問題、讓專案能繼續進行」的語境。 (D) ignore（忽視）：若工程師「忽視」這些已被發現的嚴重安全隱憂，將造成更大的危險，與句子強調「必須」(had to) 採取行動的語氣相矛盾。

3.The volunteers worked ______ throughout the night to deliver supplies to the flood victims.

(A) tirelessly
(B) reluctantly
(C) carelessly
(D) occasionally
中譯：志工們整夜＿＿＿地工作，將補給品送給洪災災民。 正解 (A) tirelessly（不知疲倦地、不辭辛勳地）：tirelessly throughout the night（整夜不知疲倦地工作）描述志工們持續、辛勤地工作以幫助災民，與「delivering supplies to flood victims」這種需要長時間、高度投入的救援行動語境完全吻合，展現志工的奉獻精神。 (B) reluctantly（不情願地）：reluctantly 表示心不甘情不願地做某事，與志工主動投入救援、整夜辛勤工作的「奉獻」精神完全相反。 (C) carelessly（粗心地）：carelessly 表示做事不謹慎、馬虎，這與「為災民運送補給」這種需要謹慎、負責態度的救援工作不符，且帶有負面意味，與整句讚揚志工的語氣不合。 (D) occasionally（偶爾）：occasionally 表示「偶爾、不時」，與「throughout the night」（整夜）所強調的「持續、不間斷」的時間概念相矛盾。

4.Many young entrepreneurs find it difficult to ______ enough capital to launch their startups.

(A) discard
(B) accumulate
(C) postpone
(D) eliminate
中譯：許多年輕的創業家發現很難＿＿＿足夠的資金來創立他們的新創公司。 正解 (B) accumulate（累積、積累）：accumulate enough capital to launch a startup（累積足夠的資金來創立新創公司）語意通順，創業家通常需要透過時間「累積」資金才能達到創業所需的金額，accumulate 與 capital（資金）搭配自然且符合創業情境的常識。 (A) discard（丟棄）：discard enough capital（丟棄足夠的資金）語意矛盾，創業家是想要「取得」資金而非「丟棄」資金，與句意完全相反。 (C) postpone（延後）：postpone enough capital 語法及語意皆不通，postpone 通常接「事件、計畫」等可延後的對象，而非「資金」這種需要被獲取的數量。 (D) eliminate（消除、去除）：eliminate enough capital（消除足夠的資金）語意不通，eliminate 表示「去除、消滅」，與「取得資金以創業」的目標完全相反。

5.The professor's lecture on climate change was so ______ that several students decided to change their majors.

(A) tedious
(B) inaudible
(C) compelling
(D) trivial
中譯：這位教授關於氣候變遷的講座如此＿＿＿，以致於有幾位學生決定改變他們的主修。 正解 (C) compelling（引人入勝的、有說服力的）：so...that...（如此...以致於...）句型中，講座必須具備某種特質才能讓學生「決定改變主修」這個重大決定，compelling（引人入勝、具說服力）正能說明講座內容極具影響力、深深打動了學生，因而促使他們做出改變人生方向的決定，語意合理且符合因果邏輯。 (A) tedious（冗長乏味的）：若講座是 tedious（乏味的），學生通常會感到無趣、想睡覺，而不會因此「決定改變主修」這種需要被強烈觸動才會做的決定，邏輯矛盾。 (B) inaudible（聽不清楚的）：inaudible 表示「聽不到、聽不清楚」，若學生連講座內容都聽不清楚，便無法理解內容進而做出改變主修的決定，邏輯不通。 (D) trivial（瑣碎的、不重要的）：trivial 表示「不重要、無關緊要」，若講座內容是瑣碎、不重要的，學生不太可能因此「決定改變主修」這樣重大的決定，語意矛盾。

6.Despite repeated warnings, the factory continued to ______ untreated waste into the river.

(A) discharge
(B) withdraw
(C) conceal
(D) absorb
中譯：儘管再三警告，這家工廠仍持續將未經處理的廢水＿＿＿到河裡。 正解 (A) discharge（排放、排出）：discharge untreated waste into the river（將未經處理的廢水排放到河裡）是描述工廠排放污染物的常見搭配，與句子描述「工廠造成污染、無視警告」的負面語境完全吻合。 (B) withdraw（撤回、提取）：withdraw waste into the river 語意不通，withdraw 通常指「撤回、收回」某事物，或從帳戶「提取」金錢，與「將廢水排入河中」這個「排放出去」的動作方向相反。 (C) conceal（隱藏、隱瞞）：conceal waste into the river 語意及搭配皆不通，conceal 表示「隱藏、藏起來」，但句子描述的是工廠將廢水「排入」河中（一種公開可見的排放行為），而非隱藏廢水。 (D) absorb（吸收）：absorb waste into the river 語意不通，absorb 表示「吸收」，主詞應是吸收廢水的物體（如河流吸收廢水），但句子主詞是「工廠」，工廠「吸收」廢水到河裡邏輯矛盾，工廠的動作應是「排放」而非「吸收」。

7.The negotiations between the two countries reached a(n) ______ after months of disagreement.

(A) obstacle
(B) deadlock
(C) compromise
(D) shortage
中譯：經過數月的意見不合後，這兩國之間的談判陷入了＿＿＿。 正解 (B) deadlock（僵局）：reached a deadlock after months of disagreement（經過數月的意見不合後陷入僵局）語意通順，deadlock 表示談判雙方因持續的分歧而無法達成共識、陷入停滯不前的狀態，與「months of disagreement」（數月的意見不合）所暗示的「談判陷入困境」完全吻合。 (A) obstacle（障礙）：reached an obstacle 搭配不自然，obstacle 通常用於「遇到」（encounter/face）某個障礙，而非「達到」(reach) 一個障礙的狀態；且 obstacle 描述的是「單一障礙物」，與「談判陷入停滯」這種整體狀態的描述不同。 (C) compromise（妥協、折衷方案）：reached a compromise（達成妥協）表示雙方各退一步、達成共識，但這與句子前述「經過數月的意見不合」(months of disagreement) 所暗示的「持續無法達成共識」的負面結果方向相反——若已達成妥協，便不會強調「意見不合」作為結果的前提。 (D) shortage（短缺）：reached a shortage 語意不通，shortage 表示「（資源等的）短缺」，與「談判」(negotiations) 的結果狀態無關，搭配不當。

8.It is ______ to assume that everyone has equal access to the internet, especially in rural regions.

(A) naive
(B) flexible
(C) thorough
(D) genuine
中譯：假設每個人都能平等地使用網路，尤其是在鄉村地區，這是＿＿＿的。 正解 (A) naive（天真的、過於單純的）：It is naive to assume that...（假設...是天真的）是常見句型，表示這種假設過於理想化、忽略了現實狀況——句子後半段點出「尤其是在鄉村地區」(especially in rural regions) 這個但書，暗示「人人都能平等使用網路」這個假設並不符合現實，naive 正好描述這種「過於單純、不切實際」的想法。 (B) flexible（有彈性的）：flexible 用於描述事物或人具有「彈性、可調整性」，與「對某個假設的評價」這個語境不符，無法形容「假設...」這件事是否合理。 (C) thorough（徹底的、全面的）：thorough 表示「做事仔細、全面」，但句子是在「評論一個假設本身是否合理」，而非評論做事方式是否周全，語意不搭。 (D) genuine（真誠的、真實的）：genuine 表示「真實、不假」，與「假設每個人都有平等網路使用機會」這個被質疑「不切實際」的論點方向相反——句子暗示這個假設「不是真的」，但 genuine 形容的對象應是「事物的真實性」而非「假設是否合理」，與 naive 的批判語氣不符。

9.The museum's new exhibit aims to ______ visitors' understanding of ancient trade routes.

(A) restrict
(B) enhance
(C) suppress
(D) postpone
中譯：這座博物館的新展覽旨在＿＿＿參觀者對古代貿易路線的理解。 正解 (B) enhance（提升、增強）：enhance visitors' understanding of ancient trade routes（提升參觀者對古代貿易路線的理解）語意通順，博物館展覽的目的通常是透過展示內容讓參觀者「增進」對某主題的認識，enhance 與 understanding 搭配自然且符合展覽的教育功能。 (A) restrict（限制）：restrict visitors' understanding（限制參觀者的理解）語意矛盾，博物館展覽的目的應是「增進」而非「限制」參觀者的知識，與展覽教育性質的本意相反。 (C) suppress（壓制、抑制）：suppress visitors' understanding（壓制參觀者的理解）同樣與展覽「促進理解」的教育目的完全相反，suppress 帶有「阻止、壓抑」的負面意味。 (D) postpone（延後）：postpone visitors' understanding 語意不通，understanding（理解）是一種認知狀態，無法被「延後」，搭配不當。

10.After the merger, the two companies had to ______ their separate computer systems into one.

(A) integrate
(B) isolate
(C) dismantle
(D) exaggerate
中譯：合併後，這兩家公司必須將各自獨立的電腦系統＿＿＿成一個。 正解 (A) integrate（整合）：integrate...into one（將...整合成一個）是常見搭配，句子描述合併後兩家公司需要將「separate computer systems」（各自獨立的系統）「整合」成單一系統，integrate 表示「將多個部分結合成一個整體」，與 into one 完美呼應。 (B) isolate（隔離、孤立）：isolate...into one 語意矛盾，isolate 表示「使...孤立、分開」，與「整合成一個」(into one) 的目標方向完全相反。 (C) dismantle（拆除、拆解）：dismantle...into one 語意不通，dismantle 表示「拆解、拆除」某事物使其不再是一個整體，與「整合成一個」(into one) 的概念相反。 (D) exaggerate（誇大）：exaggerate...into one 語意不通，exaggerate 用於描述「言語、說法上的誇大」，與「將電腦系統整合成一個」這個具體的技術整合行動完全無關。

第五部分　閱讀測驗（第35-46題）
第35至38題為題組

When most people think of the night sky, they picture stars scattered against a black background. However, for billions of people living in or near large cities, this view has become increasingly rare. Light pollution—the excessive or misdirected artificial light produced by streetlights, billboards, and buildings—has dramatically reduced the visibility of stars in urban areas. In some major cities, only a handful of the brightest stars remain visible on a clear night, compared to thousands that can be seen from a truly dark location. Beyond simply spoiling the view, light pollution has measurable effects on wildlife and human health. Many nocturnal animals, such as bats, owls, and certain insects, rely on natural darkness to hunt, navigate, and reproduce. Artificial light can disrupt these behaviors, leading to population declines in some species. Sea turtle hatchlings, for example, instinctively move toward the brightest light source after emerging from their nests, which under natural conditions would be moonlight reflecting off the ocean. Coastal lighting from hotels and roads, however, often draws hatchlings inland instead, where they face a much higher risk of death. In humans, exposure to artificial light at night has been linked to disrupted sleep patterns, as the body's internal clock relies on darkness to trigger the release of melatonin, a hormone that promotes sleep. Studies have suggested that people living in areas with high levels of light pollution report more difficulty falling asleep and experience lower-quality rest overall. Fortunately, light pollution is one of the most reversible forms of environmental damage. Unlike air or water pollution, it can be reduced almost immediately simply by changing the type, direction, and timing of outdoor lighting. A growing number of communities have adopted "dark sky" initiatives, which involve installing shielded light fixtures that direct light downward rather than into the sky, and turning off non-essential lighting after certain hours. Some of these communities have even been designated as official Dark Sky Parks, attracting visitors who travel specifically to experience a sky full of stars.

第43至46題為題組

Long before paper was invented, ancient civilizations developed ingenious methods for recording information. One of the most remarkable of these methods was the quipu, a system of knotted cords used by the Inca Empire, which flourished in South America from the 15th to the 16th century. A quipu typically consisted of a main cord from which numerous smaller, colored cords hung, each tied with a series of knots at different positions. For many years, scholars believed that quipus were used primarily for accounting purposes—recording numbers related to taxes, census data, and stored goods such as grain or textiles. The position and type of each knot represented a specific numerical value, following a system based on units of ten, similar to the decimal system used today. By examining surviving quipus, researchers have been able to confirm that the Inca had a sophisticated understanding of mathematics, capable of recording large quantities with great precision. However, more recent research suggests that quipus may have served a far more complex purpose. Some scholars argue that the color, texture, and arrangement of the cords could also encode non-numerical information, possibly including historical narratives, genealogies, or even letters sent between officials across the vast Inca road network. If this theory is correct, quipus would represent a unique writing system entirely different from anything else in the world—one based on three-dimensional knots and textures rather than symbols drawn on a flat surface. Unfortunately, when Spanish colonizers arrived in the 16th century, they viewed quipus with suspicion, associating them with indigenous religious practices they sought to eliminate. As a result, many quipus were destroyed, and the detailed knowledge required to create and interpret them was largely lost within a few generations. Today, fewer than a thousand quipus are known to survive in museums and private collections, and researchers continue to use computer analysis to search for patterns that might finally reveal the full extent of what these remarkable objects were able to record.
