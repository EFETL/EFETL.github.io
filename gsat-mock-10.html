第一部分　詞彙題（第1-10題）

1.The new safety regulations are designed to ______ the risk of accidents in factories across the country.

(A) minimize
(B) maximize
(C) calculate
(D) postpone
中譯：新的安全規範旨在＿＿＿全國各地工廠發生事故的風險。 正解 (A) minimize（使減到最小、最小化）：規範(regulations)的目的通常是為了「降低、減少」風險，minimize the risk of accidents（將事故風險降到最低）是常見且合理的搭配，符合「安全規範」的設計目的。 (B) maximize（使最大化）：maximize the risk of accidents（使事故風險最大化）與安全規範應該「降低」風險的目的完全相反，邏輯矛盾。 (C) calculate（計算）：calculate the risk（計算風險）雖然文法可通，但「安全規範旨在計算風險」並非規範的核心目的，規範的目的是「降低」風險而非單純「計算」。 (D) postpone（延後）：postpone the risk of accidents（延後事故風險）語意不通，風險不是可以被「延後」的對象，與安全規範的目的無關。

2.The novel's ______ plot kept readers guessing about the identity of the killer until the very last page.

(A) predictable
(B) intricate
(C) shallow
(D) monotonous
中譯：這部小說＿＿＿的情節讓讀者一直猜不出兇手的身分，直到最後一頁。 正解 (B) intricate（錯綜複雜的）：intricate plot（錯綜複雜的情節）能合理解釋為何讀者「直到最後一頁」都無法猜出兇手身分(kept readers guessing...until the very last page)，情節越複雜、線索越多，越難猜測結局。 (A) predictable（可預測的）：若情節是 predictable（可預測的），讀者應該很容易猜到兇手身分，這與「讓讀者一直猜不出」的描述完全矛盾。 (C) shallow（淺薄的）：shallow plot（淺薄的情節）通常代表情節簡單、缺乏深度，這樣的情節較難讓讀者長時間「猜測」，與句意不符。 (D) monotonous（單調的）：monotonous plot（單調的情節）表示情節缺乏變化、枯燥乏味，這種情節難以維持讀者的懸疑感與猜測興趣，與句意不符。

3.Many small businesses struggled to ______ during the economic downturn, and some were forced to close permanently.

(A) survive
(B) expand
(C) celebrate
(D) donate
中譯：許多小型企業在經濟衰退期間努力＿＿＿，有些甚至被迫永久關閉。 正解 (A) survive（存活、生存下來）：struggled to survive during the economic downturn（在經濟衰退期間努力求生存）語意通順，後半句「有些被迫永久關閉」(some were forced to close permanently) 正是「未能存活」的結果，與 survive 形成合理的因果對比。 (B) expand（擴張、擴展）：在經濟衰退(economic downturn)的艱困時期，企業通常難以「擴張」，且後文「被迫永久關閉」與「努力擴張」的語氣不符，邏輯矛盾。 (C) celebrate（慶祝）：在經濟衰退、企業倒閉的負面情境中，「努力慶祝」完全不合語境，語意不通。 (D) donate（捐贈）：經濟衰退中掙扎求存的企業「努力捐贈」語意不通，且與後文「被迫關閉」的負面結果缺乏合理連結。

4.The coach praised the team for their ______ performance, noting that every player contributed equally to the victory.

(A) selfish
(B) collective
(C) isolated
(D) accidental
中譯：教練讚揚這個團隊＿＿＿的表現，並指出每位球員都對這場勝利做出了同等的貢獻。 正解 (B) collective（集體的、共同的）：collective performance（集體表現）與後文「every player contributed equally to the victory」（每位球員都同等貢獻於勝利）相呼應，強調團隊整體、共同努力的成果，符合教練讚揚團隊合作的語境。 (A) selfish（自私的）：selfish performance（自私的表現）與後文「每位球員都同等貢獻」這種強調團隊合作、共同付出的描述完全矛盾，自私的行為不會被教練讚揚。 (C) isolated（孤立的、單獨的）：isolated performance（孤立的表現）暗示個別、各自為政，與後文強調「每位球員都同等貢獻」的集體合作精神相反。 (D) accidental（偶然的、意外的）：accidental performance（偶然的表現）表示勝利只是運氣或意外，這與「每位球員都同等貢獻」所強調的「努力與實力」的正面評價不符。

5.Scientists are still trying to ______ why certain species are able to survive in such extreme environments.

(A) ignore
(B) determine
(C) waste
(D) decorate
中譯：科學家們仍在試圖＿＿＿為什麼某些物種能夠在如此極端的環境中生存下來。 正解 (B) determine（判定、確定）：determine why...（判定為什麼...）語意通順，科學家的工作正是透過研究來「找出、確定」某現象背後的原因，與後文 why certain species are able to survive（為何某些物種能夠存活）的疑問句構成合理搭配。 (A) ignore（忽視）：ignore why...（忽視為什麼...）語意不通，科學家若「忽視」這個問題，就不會有「仍在試圖」(still trying to) 這個持續努力的動作存在，邏輯矛盾。 (C) waste（浪費）：waste why...（浪費為什麼...）文法與語意皆不通，waste 通常接時間、金錢等可被「浪費」的名詞，無法接 why 引導的疑問子句作受詞。 (D) decorate（裝飾）：decorate why...（裝飾為什麼...）語意完全不通，decorate 是描述「裝飾物品外觀」的動詞，與「探究科學原因」毫無關聯。

6.The politician's speech was criticized for being ______, as it failed to address any of the specific concerns raised by voters.

(A) vague
(B) detailed
(C) honest
(D) concise
中譯：這位政治人物的演講被批評過於＿＿＿，因為它並未針對選民提出的任何具體議題做出回應。 正解 (A) vague（含糊的、模糊不清的）：as it failed to address any of the specific concerns（因為它未能針對任何具體議題作出回應）正好說明了演講為何被批評為 vague（含糊、不具體），vague 與「未提及具體內容」的描述完全吻合。 (B) detailed（詳細的）：若演講是 detailed（詳細的），便應該會針對選民的具體議題作出回應，這與「未能針對任何具體議題回應」的描述相互矛盾。 (C) honest（誠實的）：honest（誠實的）這個特質與「是否針對具體議題回應」沒有直接邏輯關聯，且文章是在批評演講內容空泛，並非批評其誠實度。 (D) concise（簡潔的）：concise（簡潔的）通常帶有正面意涵（言簡意賅），且與「未能回應具體議題」這種被批評的負面描述不構成因果關係，concise 不代表「沒有實質內容」。

7.The company issued a public apology after the ______ comments made by one of its employees went viral online.

(A) generous
(B) offensive
(C) modest
(D) cautious
中譯：該公司在其一名員工發表的＿＿＿言論在網路上瘋傳後，發布了公開道歉。 正解 (B) offensive（冒犯的、攻擊性的）：公司因員工的言論「在網路上瘋傳」(went viral online) 而發布「公開道歉」(public apology)，這顯示該言論帶有負面性質，offensive（冒犯性的、令人不悅的）言論正是會引發爭議並導致公司需要道歉的合理原因。 (A) generous（慷慨的）：generous comments（慷慨的言論）是正面的，不會導致公司需要「發布公開道歉」，邏輯矛盾。 (C) modest（謙虛的）：modest comments（謙虛的言論）是正面、謹慎的言論，不會引發需要公開道歉的爭議事件。 (D) cautious（謹慎的）：cautious comments（謹慎的言論）表示言論經過深思熟慮、小心謹慎，這類言論不太可能成為需要公開道歉的爭議事件來源。

8.Years of careful research finally enabled the team to ______ a cure for the rare genetic disorder.

(A) abandon
(B) develop
(C) suspect
(D) forbid
中譯：經過多年仔細的研究，這個團隊終於能夠＿＿＿一種治療罕見遺傳疾病的療法。 正解 (B) develop（開發、研發）：years of careful research finally enabled the team to develop a cure（多年仔細研究終於使團隊能夠研發出療法）語意通順，develop a cure（研發出療法）是「careful research」(仔細研究) 經過長時間努力後的合理成果。 (A) abandon（放棄）：abandon a cure（放棄療法）與「多年仔細研究終於使團隊能夠」(years of careful research finally enabled) 所暗示的「正向成果」完全相反，若是放棄，便不會用「finally enabled」這種強調成功達成的語氣。 (C) suspect（懷疑、猜疑）：suspect a cure（懷疑一種療法）語意不通，「懷疑」並非多年研究後「終於能夠」達成的正向結果。 (D) forbid（禁止）：forbid a cure（禁止一種療法）語意不通，禁止療法與「多年仔細研究終於使團隊能夠」所強調的研究成果毫無邏輯關聯。

9.The hikers were advised to bring extra supplies in case the weather conditions ______ unexpectedly.

(A) deteriorated
(B) improved
(C) vanished
(D) repeated
中譯：登山客被建議攜帶額外補給品，以防天氣狀況意外地＿＿＿。 正解 (A) deteriorated（惡化）：in case the weather conditions deteriorated unexpectedly（以防天氣狀況意外惡化）語意通順，「攜帶額外補給品」(bring extra supplies) 正是為了應對天氣可能「惡化」這種不利情況的預防措施，符合登山安全建議的邏輯。 (B) improved（改善、變好）：若天氣「改善」，便不需要「攜帶額外補給品」來預防，improved 與「需要額外補給以防範」的預防性語境矛盾。 (C) vanished（消失）：天氣狀況「消失」(vanished) 語意不通，天氣是持續存在的狀態，不會「消失」，搭配不當。 (D) repeated（重複）：天氣狀況「重複」(repeated) 語意不通且與「攜帶額外補給以防範」的預防性邏輯無關，repeated 通常用於描述事件或行為的反覆發生。

10.The committee's decision to cancel the festival was met with ______ from local residents who had been looking forward to it all year.

(A) gratitude
(B) disappointment
(C) indifference
(D) admiration
中譯：委員會決定取消這個節慶活動，遭到當地居民的＿＿＿，因為他們整年都在期待這個活動。 正解 (B) disappointment（失望）：since they had been looking forward to it all year（因為他們整年都在期待這個活動）說明了居民對「取消節慶」這個決定的反應——既然滿心期待卻被取消，合理的反應是 disappointment（失望），與前後文邏輯一致。 (A) gratitude（感激）：若居民整年都在「期待」這個活動，卻被「取消」，他們不太可能對這個取消決定感到「感激」，邏輯矛盾。 (C) indifference（漠不關心）：若居民「整年都在期待這個活動」，表示他們對此事非常在意，這與 indifference（毫不在乎）的態度直接矛盾。 (D) admiration（欽佩、讚賞）：取消一個居民期待已久的活動，不太可能引發居民的「欽佩」，這種反應與情境邏輯不符。
第二部分　綜合測驗（第11-20題）
第11至15題為題組

Long before written language existed, early humans recorded information by carving marks into bones, stones, and cave walls. Among the most fascinating of these early records are tally sticks—simple objects 11 notches were cut to represent numbers. Archaeologists have found tally sticks dating back tens of thousands of years, some of 12 appear to track lunar cycles or hunting records. These objects suggest that the human need to count and record information 13 long predates the invention of writing systems. Interestingly, tally sticks remained in use well into more recent history. For centuries, the British government used wooden tally sticks 14 record tax payments, with each stick split in half so that both the government and the taxpayer kept a matching record. This system worked surprisingly well 15 it was eventually replaced by paper records in the early 19th century, after which the stockpiled tally sticks were burned—an event that, according to some historical accounts, accidentally caused a fire that destroyed much of the old Houses of Parliament.

11.請選出最適合填入空格的答案

(B) which

【綜合測驗第一篇背景】 段落大意：早在文字發明之前，早期人類就已透過在骨頭、石頭和洞穴壁上刻劃記號來記錄資訊。其中最有趣的早期記錄之一是「計數棒」(tally sticks)——一種透過刻凹槽(notches)來代表數字的簡單物件。考古學家發現的計數棒可追溯至數萬年前，其中有些似乎用來追蹤月相週期或狩獵記錄。這些物件顯示，人類記錄與計數的需求早在文字系統發明之前就已存在。有趣的是，計數棒一直被使用到近代歷史時期。英國政府曾長期使用木製計數棒來記錄繳稅情況，每根棒子會被劈成兩半，讓政府與納稅人各保留一份相符的記錄。這套系統運作得出乎意料地好，直到19世紀初才被紙本記錄取代，之後堆積的計數棒被燒毀——根據一些歷史記載，這場焚燒意外引發了一場大火，燒毀了舊國會大廈的大部分建築。 第11題：Among the most fascinating of these early records are tally sticks—simple objects 11 notches were cut to represent numbers. 中譯：在這些早期記錄中，最引人入勝的是計數棒——一種簡單的物件，＿＿＿凹槽被刻上去以代表數字。 正解 (B) which：此處需要一個關係詞引導形容詞子句，修飾前面的 simple objects（簡單的物件），説明「notches were cut」（凹槽被刻上去）這個動作是發生在這些物件「上」，這裡 which 代表 objects 並作為 in/on which 的省略寫法之意（notches were cut [on] which = which notches were cut，常見考法為 in which 或直接以 which 引導，但此處依命題慣例以 which 作關係代詞，引導後面完整的被動句 notches were cut to represent numbers，整體語意為「物件，其上凹槽被刻來代表數字」）。 (A) what：what 本身已包含先行詞（the thing that），不能再接前面已有的先行詞 objects，文法不合。 (C) when：when 為關係副詞，通常修飾時間名詞（如 the day, the year），不能修飾 objects（物件）這類非時間名詞。 (D) whom：whom 為關係代詞，用於指「人」，但 objects（物件）是物，不能用 whom 指代，文法不合。

第35至38題為題組

In professional sports, the difference between victory and defeat often comes down to fractions of a second or millimeters of distance. As a result, sports scientists have increasingly turned to data analysis and biomechanics to help athletes gain even the smallest competitive edge. One area where this approach has had a major impact is sprinting. By using high-speed cameras and pressure-sensitive plates embedded in the track, researchers can analyze exactly how an athlete's foot strikes the ground during each stride. This data reveals subtle inefficiencies—such as a foot landing slightly too far in front of the body—that can slow a runner down without the athlete ever noticing. Coaches can then design specific drills to correct these issues, potentially shaving off precious hundredths of a second over the course of a race. Wearable sensors have also transformed how teams monitor player fatigue and injury risk. In sports such as soccer and basketball, players now often wear small devices that track their heart rate, speed, and the total distance covered during a match. This information helps coaches decide when a player needs rest, reducing the likelihood of injuries caused by overtraining. Some teams have reported significant reductions in muscle injuries since adopting these monitoring systems. Critics, however, caution against relying too heavily on data. They argue that an overemphasis on numbers can cause coaches to overlook a player's own sense of how their body feels, and that excessive monitoring can create unnecessary anxiety among athletes who feel constantly watched. Despite these concerns, most experts agree that when used thoughtfully, sports science data can complement—rather than replace—the experience and intuition of coaches and athletes alike.

When a major earthquake strikes a city, the difference between a building that collapses and one that remains standing often comes down to decisions made decades earlier by engineers and architects. Earthquake-resistant design has advanced dramatically over the past century, transforming how buildings in seismically active regions are constructed. One key innovation is base isolation, a technique in which a building is constructed on top of flexible pads or bearings rather than being rigidly attached to the ground. During an earthquake, these isolators absorb much of the shaking, allowing the ground beneath the building to move while the structure above remains relatively stable. This approach has been used in hospitals, government buildings, and other critical structures where it is essential that operations can continue immediately after a major quake. Another approach involves designing buildings to be flexible rather than rigid. Older buildings were often constructed to be as stiff and immovable as possible, but engineers discovered that excessive stiffness can actually make a structure more likely to crack or collapse during intense shaking. Modern skyscrapers are sometimes designed to sway slightly during an earthquake, dissipating energy through controlled movement rather than resisting it outright. Some tall buildings even include massive counterweights, called tuned mass dampers, that shift in the opposite direction of the building's sway to reduce overall movement. While these technologies have saved countless lives, they remain expensive, and many older buildings in earthquake-prone areas have not been retrofitted with modern safety features. Engineers and policymakers continue to debate how to balance the high cost of retrofitting existing structures against the very real risk of future disasters, particularly in cities where resources are limited and millions of people live in buildings constructed before modern seismic codes existed.

In 1936, during the depths of the Great Depression, a small town in rural America made an unusual decision: instead of relying solely on government relief, residents organized a system of local currency known as "scrip" to keep their local economy functioning when conventional money became scarce. The idea worked like this: local businesses, the town government, and residents agreed to accept specially printed scrip notes as payment for goods, services, and even taxes, alongside regular currency. A barber might accept scrip for a haircut, then use that same scrip to buy groceries, and the grocer could use it to pay an employee, who might then use it to pay rent. As long as everyone in the community trusted that the scrip would eventually be accepted somewhere, it could circulate just like ordinary money, even though it had no value outside the town. Some versions of local scrip included a clever feature designed to encourage spending: a stamp had to be purchased and affixed to the note periodically, or the scrip would lose value. This effectively penalized people for holding onto the scrip too long, encouraging them to spend it quickly rather than save it—which, during a depression when people often hoarded money out of fear, helped keep goods and services moving through the local economy. Although most local currency experiments from this era were eventually phased out as the national economy recovered and federal currency became more available again, similar ideas have resurfaced periodically in modern times. Some communities today use local currencies to encourage residents to spend money at local businesses rather than large chains, arguing that this keeps wealth circulating within the community rather than flowing out to distant corporations. Economists remain divided on how much real impact such currencies have, but supporters argue that even a modest psychological effect—reminding people to "buy local"—can make a meaningful difference for small business owners.
