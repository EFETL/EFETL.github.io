第一部分　詞彙題（第1-10題）

1.The new traffic regulations were designed to ______ congestion in the city center during rush hour.

(A) alleviate
(B) intensify
(C) overlook
(D) duplicate
中譯：新的交通法規旨在＿＿＿市中心尖峰時段的交通壅塞情況。 正解 (A) alleviate（緩解、減輕）：alleviate congestion（緩解壅塞）是常見搭配，新法規的設計目的是「改善」交通壅塞問題，alleviate 強調「減輕程度」之意，與「designed to」（旨在）所暗示的正向改善目標完全吻合。 (B) intensify（加劇）：intensify congestion（加劇壅塞）與「新法規旨在改善交通」的正向目的完全相反。 (C) overlook（忽視）：overlook congestion（忽視壅塞）與「新法規被設計來處理交通問題」的積極作為矛盾，忽視並非法規的設計目的。 (D) duplicate（複製）：duplicate congestion（複製壅塞）語意不通，「複製交通壅塞」邏輯上講不通，與法規改善交通的目的無關。

2.Despite repeated warnings, the company continued to ______ environmental regulations until it was fined heavily.

(A) endorse
(B) violate
(C) clarify
(D) celebrate
中譯：儘管多次被警告，這家公司仍持續＿＿＿環保法規，直到被重罰才停止。 正解 (B) violate（違反）：violate environmental regulations（違反環保法規）語意通順，「continued to violate」（持續違反）與「until it was fined heavily」（直到被重罰）形成合理的因果關係——正因持續違反法規，才導致最終被重罰。 (A) endorse（支持、認可）：endorse environmental regulations（支持環保法規）與後文「直到被重罰」的負面結果矛盾——若公司一直支持並遵守法規，就不會遭到重罰。 (C) clarify（澄清）：clarify environmental regulations（澄清環保法規）語意不通，「澄清法規」並非公司的行為，且與「被重罰」的結果無因果關聯。 (D) celebrate（慶祝）：celebrate environmental regulations（慶祝環保法規）語意不通，且與「儘管多次警告」、「直到被重罰」的負面語境完全不符。

3.The young violinist's performance was so ______ that the entire audience rose to applaud.

(A) mediocre
(B) exquisite
(C) forgettable
(D) awkward
中譯：這位年輕小提琴家的演出如此＿＿＿，全場觀眾都起立鼓掌。 正解 (B) exquisite（精緻絕美的）：exquisite performance（精湛絕美的演出）能合理解釋「全場觀眾起立鼓掌」(the entire audience rose to applaud) 這個熱烈反應，exquisite 強調表演極致的美感與技巧，與正面結果呼應。 (A) mediocre（平庸的）：mediocre performance（平庸的演出）不太可能引發「全場觀眾起立鼓掌」這種熱烈反應，邏輯矛盾。 (C) forgettable（容易被遺忘的）：forgettable performance（容易被遺忘的演出）與「觀眾起立鼓掌」這種令人印象深刻的反應方向相反。 (D) awkward（笨拙的）：awkward performance（笨拙的演出）與「觀眾起立鼓掌」的熱烈讚賞反應完全矛盾。

4.After the merger, the two companies had to ______ their separate computer systems into one.

(A) integrate
(B) abandon
(C) interrupt
(D) suspend
中譯：合併之後，兩家公司必須將各自獨立的電腦系統＿＿＿成一個系統。 正解 (A) integrate（整合）：integrate...into one（將...整合成一個）是固定搭配，合併後將「separate computer systems」（各自獨立的電腦系統）整合成單一系統，integrate 完全符合「合併」(merger) 之後系統統一的邏輯。 (B) abandon（放棄）：abandon...into one 語意及語法均不通，「放棄成一個」邏輯不合，且 abandon 通常不接 into one 這種結果補語。 (C) interrupt（中斷）：interrupt...into one 語意不通，「中斷成一個」邏輯不合，interrupt 表示「打斷、使停止」，與「合併成單一系統」的目的無關。 (D) suspend（暫停）：suspend...into one 語意不通，「暫停成一個」邏輯不合，suspend 表示「暫時停止」，與「將兩個系統整合為一」的積極合併行動不符。

5.The detective's ______ observation skills allowed her to notice details that everyone else had missed.

(A) careless
(B) keen
(C) ordinary
(D) reluctant
中譯：這位偵探＿＿＿的觀察力，使她能注意到其他人都忽略的細節。 正解 (B) keen（敏銳的）：keen observation skills（敏銳的觀察力）是常見搭配，與後文「notice details that everyone else had missed」（注意到其他人都忽略的細節）完全呼應——正因觀察力敏銳，才能發現別人忽略的細節。 (A) careless（粗心的）：careless observation skills（粗心的觀察力）與「注意到別人忽略的細節」這種精準、敏銳的能力完全矛盾。 (C) ordinary（普通的）：ordinary observation skills（普通的觀察力）無法合理解釋「注意到別人都忽略的細節」這種超乎常人的能力，與句意不符。 (D) reluctant（不情願的）：reluctant 用於形容人對某事的態度（不情願去做），無法直接修飾「observation skills（觀察力）」這個能力特質，搭配不當。

6.The factory was forced to ______ production after several workers reported safety concerns.

(A) halt
(B) accelerate
(C) advertise
(D) duplicate
中譯：在數名員工反映安全顧慮後，這家工廠被迫＿＿＿生產。 正解 (A) halt（停止）：halt production（停止生產）語意通順，「員工反映安全顧慮」(reported safety concerns) 是促使工廠「被迫停止生產」(forced to halt production) 的合理原因，halt 強調「立即停止」之意，符合安全考量下緊急應變的語境。 (B) accelerate（加速）：accelerate production（加速生產）與「員工反映安全顧慮」這個應該促使「減緩或停止」生產的情境邏輯相反。 (C) advertise（宣傳）：advertise production（宣傳生產）語意不通，「宣傳生產」與「員工反映安全顧慮、工廠被迫採取行動」的因果關係無關。 (D) duplicate（複製）：duplicate production（複製生產）語意不通，與「因安全顧慮而被迫採取行動」的語境無關。

7.The charity relies entirely on ______ from generous individuals and local businesses.

(A) penalties
(B) donations
(C) debts
(D) taxes
中譯：這家慈善機構完全依賴來自慷慨人士與當地企業的＿＿＿。 正解 (B) donations（捐款、捐贈）：rely entirely on donations from generous individuals and local businesses（完全依賴來自慷慨人士與當地企業的捐款）語意通順，donations 與「generous individuals」（慷慨的人們）所暗示的「捐贈行為」完全呼應，慈善機構(charity)依賴捐款運作是合理且常見的描述。 (A) penalties（罰款、處罰）：rely on penalties from generous individuals（依賴來自慷慨人士的罰款）語意不通，「罰款」與「慷慨」(generous) 的語意方向矛盾，慈善機構不會「依賴罰款」運作。 (C) debts（債務）：rely on debts（依賴債務）語意不通，慈善機構若「依賴債務」運作，與「來自慷慨個人與企業」的描述不符，debts 通常是負債方需償還的款項，而非他人主動提供的資源。 (D) taxes（稅金）：rely on taxes from generous individuals and local businesses（依賴來自慷慨人士與當地企業的稅金）語意不通，稅金是強制性的政府收入，並非「慷慨人士」自願提供給慈善機構的資源。

8.It took years of ______ effort for the small startup to become a globally recognized brand.

(A) tireless
(B) accidental
(C) brief
(D) careless
中譯：這家小型新創公司花了數年＿＿＿的努力，才成為全球知名品牌。 正解 (A) tireless（不知疲倦的、不懈的）：tireless effort（不懈的努力）語意通順，「years of tireless effort」（數年不懈的努力）能合理解釋小型新創公司「成為全球知名品牌」(become a globally recognized brand) 這個重大成就的取得過程。 (B) accidental（意外的）：accidental effort（意外的努力）語意矛盾，「努力」本身通常是有意識、刻意的行為，accidental（意外、偶然）與「effort」搭配不自然，且與「花費數年」這種長期、刻意的投入不符。 (C) brief（短暫的）：years of brief effort（數年的短暫努力）語意自相矛盾，「years」（數年）暗示長期投入，與「brief」（短暫的）直接衝突。 (D) careless（粗心的）：careless effort（粗心的努力）與「成為全球知名品牌」這種需要謹慎經營、長期累積的成就方向不符，粗心的努力難以達成如此重大的成果。

9.The novel's plot is so ______ that readers often need to take notes to keep track of the characters.

(A) simple
(B) intricate
(C) dull
(D) predictable
中譯：這部小說的情節如此＿＿＿，讀者經常需要做筆記才能跟上角色的脈絡。 正解 (B) intricate（複雜精細的）：intricate plot（複雜精細的情節）能合理解釋「讀者需要做筆記才能跟上角色」(readers often need to take notes to keep track of the characters) 這個結果——正因情節錯綜複雜，讀者才需要額外的輔助方式來理解故事，符合「so...that」的因果句型。 (A) simple（簡單的）：simple plot（簡單的情節）與「讀者需要做筆記才能跟上」這種需要額外努力理解的情況矛盾——簡單的情節通常不需要做筆記。 (C) dull（乏味的）：dull plot（乏味的情節）無法合理解釋「讀者需要做筆記跟上角色」這個結果，乏味的情節通常令人興趣缺缺，而非因「複雜」而需要記錄。 (D) predictable（可預測的）：predictable plot（可預測的情節）與「讀者需要做筆記才能跟上」的情況矛盾——可預測的情節通常容易理解，不需要額外的記錄輔助。

10.Local residents have voiced strong ______ to the construction of a new airport near their homes.

(A) opposition
(B) approval
(C) indifference
(D) admiration
中譯：當地居民對在他們家附近建造新機場一事表達了強烈的＿＿＿。 正解 (A) opposition（反對）：voiced strong opposition to the construction（表達強烈的反對）語意通順，「local residents」（當地居民）對「在自家附近建機場」表達「強烈反對」是合理且常見的反應，opposition 與 to 搭配構成「對...的反對」這一固定用法。 (B) approval（贊同、批准）：voiced strong approval（表達強烈的贊同）與一般居民對「在住家附近建機場」（通常帶來噪音、環境影響等問題）的負面反應方向不符，approval 偏向正向支持，與一般情境下居民的擔憂相反。 (C) indifference（無所謂、冷漠）：voiced strong indifference（表達強烈的冷漠）語意自相矛盾——「strong」（強烈的）與「indifference」（冷漠、不在乎）兩者語意衝突，無法並存。 (D) admiration（欽佩、讚賞）：voiced strong admiration（表達強烈的欽佩）與居民對「機場建造在自家附近」可能帶來的困擾（噪音、交通等）的一般反應方向不符，admiration 通常用於正向、令人讚賞的事物。

For centuries, maps were drawn by hand, often based on incomplete information gathered by explorers, sailors, and surveyors. Many early maps contained significant errors, blank spaces labeled "unknown," and even imaginary islands that later turned out not to exist. Today, satellite imagery and GPS technology have transformed cartography, allowing maps to be updated almost instantly and with remarkable precision. One of the most significant shifts in modern mapping is the move from static, printed maps to dynamic digital ones. Services that provide turn-by-turn navigation rely on a constant stream of data, including real-time traffic conditions, road closures, and even crowd-sourced reports from other drivers. This means that the "map" most people use today is not a fixed document but a living system that changes by the minute. Digital mapping has also opened the door to entirely new types of maps. Researchers can now create maps showing the spread of diseases, patterns of internet usage, or even the emotional tone of social media posts in different regions. These maps help scientists, governments, and businesses understand patterns that would be invisible on a traditional paper map. However, the shift to digital maps raises new questions. Because so much mapping data is collected through smartphones, areas with fewer smartphone users—often poorer or more rural regions—may be less accurately represented. Some critics worry that this could create a feedback loop, where well-mapped areas receive more attention, investment, and services, while under-mapped areas continue to be overlooked. As mapping technology continues to advance, ensuring that all regions are represented fairly remains an important challenge.

Coffee is one of the most widely consumed beverages in the world, but the journey from coffee plant to cup is far more complex than most drinkers realize. Coffee plants typically take three to five years to produce their first harvest, and even then, the cherries—the fruit that contains the coffee beans—must be picked at exactly the right moment. Cherries that are picked too early or too late can result in beans with undesirable flavors. After harvesting, the cherries must be processed to remove the outer fruit and reveal the beans inside. There are several methods of processing, each of which affects the final flavor of the coffee. In the "washed" method, the fruit is removed before the beans are dried, often resulting in a cleaner, brighter taste. In the "natural" method, the cherries are dried with the fruit still attached, allowing sugars from the fruit to be absorbed into the bean, producing a sweeter, fruitier flavor. Once dried, the beans are still green and have little aroma. It is only through roasting that coffee develops its familiar smell and taste. Roasting involves heating the beans to high temperatures for a precise amount of time, triggering chemical reactions that create hundreds of distinct flavor compounds. Even small differences in roasting time or temperature can dramatically change the final flavor, which is why skilled roasters are highly valued in the industry. Despite the popularity of coffee, the farmers who grow it often receive only a small fraction of the final retail price. This has led to the growth of certification programs that aim to ensure farmers receive fair compensation, as well as a growing market for specialty coffee, where consumers pay premium prices for beans with traceable origins and higher quality standards.

In 1969, a group of researchers connected four computers at different universities, creating a network that would eventually become the foundation of the modern internet. At the time, few could have imagined that this experimental project, known as ARPANET, would one day connect billions of devices across the globe. The original goal was relatively modest: to allow researchers at different institutions to share computing resources and communicate more efficiently. Throughout the 1970s and 1980s, the network grew steadily, but it remained largely confined to universities, government agencies, and research institutions. A major turning point came in 1989, when British computer scientist Tim Berners-Lee proposed a new system for organizing and linking documents across computers, which he called the World Wide Web. Unlike earlier systems that required specialized knowledge to use, the Web introduced simple addresses and clickable links, making it accessible to ordinary people for the first time. The 1990s saw explosive growth as personal computers became more affordable and internet service providers began offering connections to households. Early websites were simple, often consisting of little more than text and basic images, but they laid the groundwork for the interactive, multimedia-rich internet we use today. The introduction of search engines made it possible to find information among the rapidly growing number of websites, while email transformed how people communicated across long distances. Looking back, it is remarkable how quickly the internet has reshaped nearly every aspect of daily life, from how people shop and work to how they form relationships and access information. Yet many of the technologies we now consider essential began as small experiments by researchers who were simply trying to solve practical problems, with little idea of the global transformation their work would eventually set in motion.
