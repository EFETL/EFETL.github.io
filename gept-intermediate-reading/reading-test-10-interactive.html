Why the Sky Is Blue

Have you ever wondered why the daytime sky looks blue? Sunlight may seem white, but it is actually 11 of many colors. As sunlight enters the air, tiny gas molecules scatter it in all directions. Blue light has shorter waves, so it is scattered much 12 than other colors. This is why blue seems to fill the whole sky. At sunset, however, the light must 13 through more air before reaching our eyes. Most of the blue is scattered away, so we 14 see warm reds and oranges instead. Understanding this simple science helps us 15 the beauty of nature every day.

The Deepest Place on Earth

Far beneath the Pacific Ocean lies the Mariana Trench, the deepest known point on our planet. At almost 11,000 meters down, it is deeper than Mount Everest is tall. The water there is freezing cold, and the pressure is strong enough to crush most machines.

For a long time, people thought nothing could live in such a dark, harsh place. Yet modern cameras have shown strange fish and tiny creatures moving along the sea floor. These discoveries remind us that life can survive in surprising conditions, and that much of the ocean is still waiting to be explored.
