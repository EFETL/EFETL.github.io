第一部分　詞彙題（第1-10題）

1.The ancient ruins were so well ______ that archaeologists could still read the inscriptions after thousands of years.

(A) preserved
(B) demolished
(C) scattered
(D) forgotten
中譯：這些古代遺跡保存得如此完好，考古學家在數千年後仍能讀懂上面的刻文。 正解 (A) preserved（保存）：so well preserved that...（保存得如此完好，以致於...）語意通順，與後文「考古學家仍能讀懂刻文」相呼應，表示遺跡狀態良好、未遭破壞，符合「保存良好」的語境。 (B) demolished（拆除、摧毀）：若遺跡被「拆除」，便不可能還留有刻文供考古學家閱讀，與後文邏輯矛盾。 (C) scattered（散落、分散）：scattered 表示物體散落各處、雜亂無序，這與「能清楚讀懂刻文」所需的完整性、清晰度不符。 (D) forgotten（被遺忘的）：forgotten 描述的是「不被記得」的狀態，與遺跡本身是否「保存完好」無關，且「被遺忘」與「考古學家仍能讀懂」沒有直接的因果關係。

2.The professor's lecture on quantum physics was so ______ that even advanced students struggled to follow it.

(A) tedious
(B) abstract
(C) casual
(D) brief
中譯：這位教授關於量子物理的講座如此＿＿＿，即使是高階學生也很難跟上。 正解 (B) abstract（抽象的）：abstract 表示概念性、難以具體理解的，量子物理本身就以抽象、難懂著稱，so abstract that even advanced students struggled to follow it（如此抽象，連高階學生都難以跟上）語意連貫合理。 (A) tedious（冗長乏味的）：tedious 強調「無聊、令人厭煩」，與「難以跟上」(struggled to follow) 所強調的「理解困難」並非同一概念，乏味不必然導致跟不上。 (C) casual（隨意的、非正式的）：casual 表示輕鬆隨意的風格，與「even advanced students struggled to follow it」所暗示的「內容艱深」完全相反。 (D) brief（簡短的）：brief 表示時間短、內容簡略，但「簡短」的講座理論上應該更容易跟上，而非「難以跟上」，邏輯矛盾。

3.The company issued a public apology after its advertisement was criticized for being ______ toward a certain group of people.

(A) inclusive
(B) generous
(C) offensive
(D) modest
中譯：該公司因其廣告被批評對某特定群體帶有＿＿＿而發出公開道歉。 正解 (C) offensive（無禮的、冒犯的）：criticized for being offensive toward a certain group of people（因對某群體帶有冒犯性而被批評）語意通順，與後文「發出公開道歉」(issued a public apology) 形成合理的因果關係——正因為廣告冒犯了某群體，公司才需要道歉。 (A) inclusive（包容的）：inclusive 表示「具包容性」，是正面特質，公司不會因廣告「具包容性」而道歉，邏輯矛盾。 (B) generous（慷慨的）：generous 是正面特質，與「遭批評」及「公開道歉」的負面情境不符。 (D) modest（謙虛的、適度的）：modest 也是正面或中性特質，與廣告「遭批評」進而「道歉」的負面語境不搭配。

4.Years of careful planning ______ the success of the city's new public transportation system.

(A) contributed to
(B) interfered with
(C) distracted from
(D) competed with
中譯：多年來細心的規劃＿＿＿這座城市新大眾運輸系統的成功。 正解 (A) contributed to（促成、有助於）：Years of careful planning contributed to the success（多年細心的規劃促成了成功）語意通順，contribute to 表示「對…有貢獻、促成」，與「careful planning」（細心規劃）和「success」（成功）之間的正向因果關係完全吻合。 (B) interfered with（干擾、妨礙）：interfered with the success（干擾了成功）語意矛盾——細心規劃應該是「促成」而非「妨礙」成功。 (C) distracted from（使分心、轉移注意力）：distracted from the success 語意不通，「使人從成功中分心」邏輯不合理，且 distract 通常用於描述「人」的注意力，而非「規劃」對「成功」的影響。 (D) competed with（與…競爭）：competed with the success 語意不通，「規劃」與「成功」之間並非競爭關係，而是促成關係。

5.The young athlete's ______ to win the gold medal motivated her to train every day, even on holidays.

(A) determination
(B) curiosity
(C) confusion
(D) hesitation
中譯：這位年輕運動員獲得金牌的＿＿＿激勵她每天訓練，即使在假日也不例外。 正解 (A) determination（決心、毅力）：determination to win the gold medal（獲得金牌的決心）語意通順，「決心」是一種強烈的內在動力，能合理解釋為何她「即使在假日也每天訓練」(motivated her to train every day, even on holidays) 這種堅持不懈的行為。 (B) curiosity（好奇心）：curiosity to win the gold medal 語意不通，「對贏得金牌的好奇心」邏輯牽強，好奇心通常是針對未知事物，而非明確的目標。 (C) confusion（困惑）：confusion to win the gold medal 語意不通，且「困惑」是一種負面、令人猶豫不前的心理狀態，無法解釋「每天努力訓練」這種積極行為。 (D) hesitation（猶豫）：hesitation to win the gold medal 語意不通，「猶豫」表示遲疑不前，與「每天努力訓練」這種堅定、積極的行動完全相反。

6.The witness gave a ______ account of the accident, including the exact time and the color of the car.

(A) vague
(B) detailed
(C) brief
(D) careless
中譯：目擊者對這場事故提供了＿＿＿的描述，包括確切的時間與車子的顏色。 正解 (B) detailed（詳細的）：a detailed account...including the exact time and the color of the car（詳細的描述，包括確切時間與車子顏色）語意通順，「確切時間」與「車子顏色」這些具體資訊正是「詳細描述」的具體例證。 (A) vague（模糊的）：vague account（模糊的描述）與後文列舉的「確切時間、車子顏色」這些具體細節完全矛盾——模糊的描述不會包含如此精確的資訊。 (C) brief（簡短的）：brief account 表示簡略的描述，但後文列舉了多項具體細節，與「簡短」的概念不符。 (D) careless（草率的、粗心的）：careless account（草率的描述）通常意味著資訊不準確或遺漏重要細節，與「包括確切時間和顏色」這種精確、用心的描述相矛盾。

7.Many small businesses struggled to ______ during the economic downturn, and some were forced to close.

(A) collapse
(B) survive
(C) expand
(D) celebrate
中譯：許多小型企業在經濟衰退期間努力＿＿＿，有些甚至被迫關閉。 正解 (B) survive（生存、存活）：struggled to survive during the economic downturn, and some were forced to close（在經濟衰退期間努力求生存，有些甚至被迫關閉）語意通順，「努力求生存」與後文「被迫關閉」形成合理的對比——有些企業努力求生存但仍以失敗（關閉）告終。 (A) collapse（崩潰、倒閉）：struggled to collapse（努力倒閉）語意矛盾，企業不會「努力地」讓自己倒閉，這與常理相反。 (C) expand（擴張）：struggled to expand during the economic downturn 雖然文法可通，但與後文「有些被迫關閉」的整體負面語境不太連貫——在經濟衰退中「努力擴張」與「被迫關閉」的對比不如「努力求生存卻仍關閉」來得自然合理。 (D) celebrate（慶祝）：struggled to celebrate（努力慶祝）語意不通，「慶祝」是正向、輕鬆的行為，與「economic downturn」（經濟衰退）及「forced to close」（被迫關閉）的負面語境完全不搭。

8.The new regulation will ______ companies from dumping waste directly into rivers and lakes.

(A) encourage
(B) permit
(C) prohibit
(D) reward
中譯：這項新法規將＿＿＿企業將廢棄物直接傾倒入河流與湖泊。 正解 (C) prohibit（禁止）：will prohibit companies from dumping waste directly into rivers and lakes（將禁止企業將廢棄物直接傾倒入河流與湖泊）語意通順，prohibit...from doing sth. 是固定搭配「禁止…做某事」，與「new regulation」（新法規）這種具約束力的規範性質完全吻合。 (A) encourage（鼓勵）：encourage companies from dumping waste 文法搭配錯誤（encourage 通常接 to do，而非 from doing），且語意上「鼓勵企業傾倒廢棄物」與保護環境的法規目的完全相反。 (B) permit（允許）：permit companies from dumping waste 文法搭配錯誤（permit 通常接 to do，而非 from doing），且「允許企業傾倒廢棄物」與法規應有的限制、保護環境目的相反。 (D) reward（獎勵）：reward companies from dumping waste 文法及語意皆不通，「獎勵企業傾倒廢棄物」與法規保護環境的目的完全矛盾。

9.Her ______ remarks during the meeting offended several colleagues, even though she claimed she was only joking.

(A) tactless
(B) thoughtful
(C) polite
(D) humble
中譯：她在會議中＿＿＿的言論冒犯了幾位同事，即使她聲稱自己只是在開玩笑。 正解 (A) tactless（不得體的、欠缺考慮的）：tactless remarks...offended several colleagues（不得體的言論冒犯了幾位同事）語意通順，「不得體」的言論正好會「冒犯」(offended) 他人，與後文的因果關係吻合。 (B) thoughtful（體貼的、深思熟慮的）：thoughtful remarks（體貼的言論）通常不會「冒犯」他人，與後文 offended several colleagues 語意矛盾。 (C) polite（禮貌的）：polite remarks（禮貌的言論）同樣不會冒犯他人，與「offended」（冒犯）的結果相反。 (D) humble（謙虛的）：humble remarks（謙虛的言論）一般也不會引起他人反感，與「冒犯了幾位同事」的結果不符。

(B) distribute
(C) discard
(D) conceal

第35至38題為題組

When most people think of bioluminescence, they imagine deep-sea creatures glowing in the darkness of the ocean. However, this remarkable ability to produce light is not limited to marine life. On land, certain species of fungi, insects, and even earthworms are capable of producing their own light through chemical reactions within their bodies. The process behind bioluminescence involves a molecule called luciferin, which reacts with oxygen in the presence of an enzyme called luciferase. This reaction releases energy in the form of light rather than heat, making it remarkably efficient compared to artificial lighting, which often wastes much of its energy as heat. Fireflies are perhaps the most familiar example of bioluminescence to people living on land. Male fireflies use flashing patterns of light to attract mates, with each species having its own distinct flashing rhythm. Female fireflies of some species have even been known to mimic the flash patterns of other species in order to lure and prey upon unsuspecting males. Glowing fungi, sometimes called "foxfire," have been documented for centuries, with early reports describing eerie green light emanating from rotting logs in forests at night. Scientists believe that, in some species, this glow may attract insects that help spread the fungus's spores. Researchers are increasingly interested in bioluminescence not only for its ecological roles but also for its potential applications. The genes responsible for producing light in certain organisms have been used in laboratories as markers to track the activity of other genes, helping scientists better understand processes inside living cells. Some researchers have even proposed using bioluminescent organisms or genetically modified plants as a natural, energy-free source of light for streets or pathways, though such applications remain largely experimental for now.

In the early 20th century, a series of devastating dust storms swept across the Great Plains of the United States, an event now known as the Dust Bowl. The disaster was caused by a combination of severe drought and farming practices that left the soil vulnerable to erosion. For decades, farmers in the region had plowed up native grasses to plant wheat and other crops, removing the deep root systems that had once held the soil in place. When a prolonged drought struck in the 1930s, the exposed topsoil dried out and turned to dust. Millions of trees were planted across the Great Plains in an effort to reduce wind erosion. The Dust Bowl remains one of the most significant environmental disasters in American history, and it continues to serve as a cautionary tale about the dangers of unsustainable land use. Many of the soil conservation practices developed in response to the Dust Bowl are still used by farmers today.
