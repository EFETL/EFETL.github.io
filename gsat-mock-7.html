第一部分　詞彙題（第1-10題）

1.The therapist encouraged patients to ______ their feelings openly rather than keeping them bottled up inside.

(A) suppress
(B) express
(C) disguise
(D) ignore
中譯：心理治療師鼓勵病人公開地＿＿＿自己的情緒，而不是將其壓抑在心裡。 正解 (B) express（表達）：rather than keeping them bottled up inside（而不是將情緒悶在心裡）與「openly express their feelings」（公開表達情緒）形成對比，治療師鼓勵病人「表達」情緒以避免壓抑，語意通順。 (A) suppress（壓抑）：suppress 與後文 bottled up（壓抑、悶住）意思重複，且與「rather than」所表達的對比邏輯矛盾——治療師鼓勵的應是與「壓抑」相反的行為。 (C) disguise（掩飾、偽裝）：disguise feelings（掩飾情緒）表示隱藏真實情緒、表現出不同的樣子，這與治療師希望病人「openly」（公開、坦誠）面對情緒的目標相反。 (D) ignore（忽視）：ignore feelings（忽視情緒）表示不去理會情緒，這也與「rather than keeping them bottled up」所暗示的「應正面處理情緒」的方向不符。

2.The archaeologists were ______ when they unearthed a perfectly preserved mosaic beneath the ancient marketplace.

(A) astonished
(B) bored
(C) annoyed
(D) suspicious
中譯：考古學家們在古代市集遺址下方挖出一幅保存完好的鑲嵌畫時感到＿＿＿。 正解 (A) astonished（驚訝的）：unearthed a perfectly preserved mosaic（挖出一幅保存完好的鑲嵌畫）是一項出乎意料的重大發現，考古學家對此感到「astonished」（驚訝、驚奇）合乎情理。 (B) bored（無聊的）：發現一幅保存完好的古代鑲嵌畫是令人興奮的重大事件，與「bored」（無聊）的情緒完全不符。 (C) annoyed（惱怒的）：挖到珍貴的考古文物並非令人生氣的事，annoyed 的負面情緒與此正向發現的語境不合。 (D) suspicious（懷疑的）：suspicious 表示心存懷疑、不信任，但句子描述的是考古學家對「重大發現」的反應，並非對某事物的真實性產生懷疑，語意不符。

3.The new headphones are designed to ______ background noise, allowing users to focus on their music.

(A) amplify
(B) eliminate
(C) detect
(D) reproduce
中譯：這款新耳機的設計目的是＿＿＿背景噪音，讓使用者能專注於音樂。 正解 (B) eliminate（消除）：headphones designed to eliminate background noise（設計用來消除背景噪音的耳機）正是「降噪耳機」(noise-cancelling headphones) 的功能描述，與後文「allowing users to focus on their music」（讓使用者能專注於音樂）邏輯一致——消除噪音才能讓人專注於音樂。 (A) amplify（放大）：amplify background noise（放大背景噪音）與「讓使用者專注於音樂」的目的完全相反，放大噪音只會讓人更難專注。 (C) detect（偵測）：detect background noise（偵測背景噪音）僅表示「察覺」噪音的存在，並未說明耳機如何處理噪音以「讓使用者專注於音樂」，語意不夠完整且不符合耳機設計的核心訴求。 (D) reproduce（重現、複製）：reproduce background noise（重現背景噪音）表示耳機會「製造出」背景噪音，這與「讓使用者專注於音樂」的目標相互矛盾。

4.Although the hikers were exhausted, they remained ______ to reach the summit before sunset.

(A) determined
(B) hesitant
(C) indifferent
(D) reluctant
中譯：儘管登山者已經疲憊不堪，他們仍然＿＿＿要在日落前抵達山頂。 正解 (A) determined（堅決的）：Although the hikers were exhausted（儘管登山者已經疲憊），they remained ______ to reach the summit（他們仍然＿＿＿要抵達山頂），although 表示讓步轉折，「疲憊」與「堅決要達成目標」形成對比，determined to（堅決要做某事）是常見搭配，語意通順。 (B) hesitant（猶豫的）：hesitant to reach the summit（猶豫是否要登頂）與「儘管疲憊仍要達成目標」的堅毅語氣不符，hesitant 表現的是遲疑不決，而非堅持到底。 (C) indifferent（不在乎的）：indifferent to reach the summit 文法搭配不自然（indifferent 通常接 to + 名詞表示「對…不在乎」），且「不在乎是否登頂」與句子強調的「儘管疲憊仍堅持」的語境矛盾。 (D) reluctant（不情願的）：reluctant to reach the summit（不情願抵達山頂）同樣與「儘管疲憊仍堅持」的正向、堅定語氣相反。

5.The professor's lecture was so ______ that several students struggled to stay awake.

(A) fascinating
(B) monotonous
(C) controversial
(D) concise
中譯：這位教授的演講如此＿＿＿，以至於好幾位學生都很難保持清醒。 正解 (B) monotonous（單調乏味的）：so ______ that several students struggled to stay awake（如此＿＿＿以至於好幾位學生都很難保持清醒），so...that 表示「如此...以至於」的因果關係，演講「單調乏味」正是導致學生難以保持清醒、容易打瞌睡的合理原因。 (A) fascinating（迷人的、引人入勝的）：fascinating lecture（引人入勝的演講）通常會讓學生更專注、更感興趣，而不會導致學生「struggled to stay awake」（很難保持清醒），語意矛盾。 (C) controversial（有爭議的）：controversial lecture（有爭議的演講）通常會引起學生的討論或情緒反應，而非讓人想睡覺，與「struggled to stay awake」的結果不符。 (D) concise（簡潔的）：concise lecture（簡潔的演講）表示演講內容精簡扼要，這通常不會讓人感到無聊想睡，與「難以保持清醒」的結果邏輯不符。

6.The city government plans to ______ the old train station into a modern art gallery.

(A) demolish
(B) convert
(C) abandon
(D) relocate
中譯：市政府計畫將老舊的火車站＿＿＿成一座現代藝術畫廊。 正解 (B) convert（轉變、改建）：convert A into B（將A轉變/改建成B）是固定搭配，convert the old train station into a modern art gallery（將老舊火車站改建成現代藝術畫廊）語意通順，描述「將既有建築物改變用途」的常見都市更新計畫。 (A) demolish（拆除）：demolish...into（拆除...成為）文法搭配不通，demolish 表示「拆毀」，拆毀後不會直接「成為」畫廊，且 demolish into 並非常見搭配。 (C) abandon（放棄、廢棄）：abandon...into 文法不通，abandon 表示「放棄不用」，與「into a modern art gallery」（變成一座現代藝術畫廊）這種「轉變為新用途」的語意不符。 (D) relocate（搬遷、遷移）：relocate...into 搭配不自然，relocate 通常指「將某物/某人遷移到別處」，而非「將建築物改變用途」，與「改建成畫廊」的語意不同。

7.Wearing a seatbelt can significantly ______ the risk of serious injury in a car accident.

(A) increase
(B) ignore
(C) reduce
(D) cause
中譯：繫安全帶能顯著＿＿＿車禍中受到重傷的風險。 正解 (C) reduce（降低、減少）：Wearing a seatbelt can significantly reduce the risk of serious injury（繫安全帶能顯著降低重傷的風險）語意通順，這是繫安全帶的核心功能與普遍常識——降低受傷風險。 (A) increase（增加）：繫安全帶會「增加」受傷風險，這與安全帶的實際功能及常識完全相反。 (B) ignore（忽視）：ignore the risk（忽視風險）表示對風險視而不見，這並非「繫安全帶」這個動作所產生的「結果」，語意不符——繫安全帶是主動降低風險的行為，而非忽視風險。 (D) cause（造成）：繫安全帶「造成」受傷風險，這與安全帶保護乘客、降低受傷機率的實際功能相反，語意矛盾。

8.The witness gave a ______ account of the accident, including details that others had missed.

(A) vague
(B) detailed
(C) brief
(D) false
中譯：目擊者對這場事故提供了＿＿＿的描述，其中包含了其他人都沒注意到的細節。 正解 (B) detailed（詳細的）：a ______ account...including details that others had missed（一份＿＿＿的描述，其中包含其他人都沒注意到的細節），「detailed account」（詳細的描述）與後文「包含其他人沒注意到的細節」相呼應——正因為描述「詳細」，才能涵蓋他人遺漏的細節。 (A) vague（模糊的）：vague account（模糊的描述）通常缺乏具體細節，這與後文「包含其他人都沒注意到的細節」（即內容非常具體、詳盡）的描述相互矛盾。 (C) brief（簡短的）：brief account（簡短的描述）暗示內容精簡、省略細節，這與「包含其他人都沒注意到的細節」所強調的「資訊豐富、詳盡」相矛盾。 (D) false（虛假的）：false account（虛假的描述）表示目擊者提供的資訊不真實，但句子並未暗示描述內容有誤，且「包含他人沒注意到的細節」更強調描述的「正確且詳盡」，與「虛假」的語意不符。

9.Many companies now ______ employees to work from home several days a week.

(A) forbid
(B) discourage
(C) permit
(D) prevent
中譯：現在許多公司＿＿＿員工每週在家工作幾天。 正解 (C) permit（允許）：Many companies now ______ employees to work from home（許多公司現在＿＿＿員工在家工作），permit/allow somebody to do something（允許某人做某事）是固定搭配，permit employees to work from home（允許員工在家工作）語意通順，符合現今企業彈性工作趨勢的描述。 (A) forbid（禁止）：forbid employees to work from home（禁止員工在家工作）與句子描述「許多公司現在...讓員工每週在家工作幾天」這一正向、開放的趨勢相反。 (B) discourage（勸阻、不鼓勵）：discourage employees to work from home 文法搭配錯誤（discourage 後面應接 from + 動名詞，即 discourage...from working），且 discourage（勸阻）的語意也與「員工確實每週在家工作幾天」這一既成事實的描述矛盾。 (D) prevent（阻止）：prevent employees to work from home 文法搭配錯誤（prevent 後面應接 from + 動名詞），且 prevent（阻止）同樣與句子描述的「員工確實能在家工作」的正向事實相矛盾。

10.The negotiations between the two countries reached a(n) ______ after months of disagreement.

(A) standstill
(B) compromise
(C) collapse
(D) dispute
中譯：經過數月的意見分歧後，兩國之間的談判達成了一個＿＿＿。 正解 (B) compromise（妥協、折衷方案）：The negotiations...reached a(n) ______ after months of disagreement（經過數月的意見分歧後，談判達成了一個＿＿＿），「reach a compromise」（達成妥協/折衷方案）是固定搭配，表示雙方在經過爭執後各退一步達成協議，與「after months of disagreement」（經過數月分歧後）的脈絡相符，呈現出「化解分歧、達成共識」的正向結果。 (A) standstill（停滯狀態）：reach a standstill（陷入停滯/僵局）表示談判「卡住、毫無進展」，這與「經過數月分歧後」可能導向的某種結果雖然合理，但 standstill 描述的是「持續的僵局」，而非「達成」(reached) 某種解決方案的正向結果，語意較不適合。 (C) collapse（崩潰、破裂）：reach a collapse 搭配不自然（collapse 通常用 end in collapse 或 the talks collapsed），且「達成一個崩潰」語意矛盾——崩潰代表談判破裂、失敗，而非「達成」某個結果。 (D) dispute（爭端、爭議）：reach a dispute 語意不通，「達成一個爭端」邏輯矛盾——dispute（爭端）本身就是「意見分歧」(disagreement) 的同義詞，談判的目的是解決爭端而非「達成」爭端。

第35至38題為題組

In 1969, a group of computer scientists at four American universities connected their computers through a network called ARPANET, laying the groundwork for what would eventually become the internet. At the time, few could have imagined that this experimental project, designed mainly to allow researchers to share information and computing resources, would one day connect billions of people across the globe. For its first two decades, the internet remained largely confined to universities, government agencies, and the military. Ordinary people had little reason or opportunity to use it. This changed dramatically in the early 1990s with the invention of the World Wide Web by British computer scientist Tim Berners-Lee. Unlike earlier systems that required specialized knowledge to navigate, the Web introduced a simple way to link documents together using hyperlinks, making it possible for anyone with a computer and a modem to browse information stored on servers around the world. The arrival of user-friendly web browsers in the mid-1990s sparked explosive growth. Businesses rushed to establish an online presence, online shopping and email became part of daily routines for millions of people, and entirely new industries emerged almost overnight. The early 2000s brought another transformation with the rise of social media platforms, which shifted the internet from something people primarily used to find information into something they used to communicate, share, and create content themselves. Today, the internet touches nearly every aspect of modern life, from how people work and shop to how they form relationships and access news. Yet this rapid growth has also raised concerns about privacy, misinformation, and the concentration of power among a small number of technology companies. As the internet continues to evolve, with developments such as artificial intelligence and virtual reality reshaping how people interact with it, questions about how to balance its benefits with its risks remain as relevant as ever.

For decades, the image of a coral reef has been one of vibrant color—brilliant blues, oranges, and greens teeming with fish of every shape and size. But in recent years, scientists have documented a troubling phenomenon known as coral bleaching, in which reefs lose their color and turn ghostly white. Coral itself is not a plant but a colony of tiny animals called polyps. These polyps have a close relationship with microscopic algae that live inside their tissues. The algae perform photosynthesis and provide the coral with food, while also giving the coral its vivid colors. In return, the coral provides the algae with a protected place to live and nutrients they need to survive. When water temperatures rise even slightly above normal for an extended period, this delicate relationship breaks down. Stressed corals expel the algae living inside them, causing the coral to turn white—a process called bleaching. Bleached coral is not dead, but it is in serious danger. Without the algae, the coral loses its main source of food and becomes far more vulnerable to disease. If conditions do not improve and the algae do not return within a few weeks, the coral is likely to die. Mass bleaching events, in which large areas of reef are affected simultaneously, have become increasingly common as ocean temperatures rise due to climate change. The Great Barrier Reef off the coast of Australia, one of the largest reef systems in the world, has experienced several major bleaching events in recent years, with some sections losing significant portions of their coral cover. Scientists are exploring various strategies to help reefs survive, including breeding corals that are more resistant to heat and creating protected marine areas where reefs face fewer additional stresses. However, most researchers agree that addressing the root cause—rising global temperatures—is essential if coral reefs are to survive in the long term.

Long before clocks and calendars as we know them existed, ancient civilizations developed remarkably sophisticated methods for tracking time, often by observing the movements of the sun, moon, and stars. One of the most famous examples is Stonehenge, a circular arrangement of massive stones in southern England built in stages over thousands of years, beginning around 3000 BC. For centuries, the purpose of Stonehenge puzzled historians and archaeologists. Various theories proposed that it was a temple, a burial ground, or even a site for healing ceremonies. One of the most widely accepted theories today is that Stonehenge functioned, at least in part, as an astronomical calendar. The stones are aligned in such a way that on the summer solstice, the longest day of the year, the rising sun appears to align directly with certain stones when viewed from the center of the monument. A similar alignment occurs during the winter solstice, the shortest day of the year. Such alignments would have been enormously useful to ancient farming communities. Knowing precisely when the solstices occurred would have helped people determine the best times to plant and harvest crops, as well as mark important seasonal festivals. Building a monument capable of tracking these events with such precision would have required considerable astronomical knowledge, careful planning, and an enormous amount of coordinated labor—some of the stones used at Stonehenge weigh over 25 tons and were transported from quarries many kilometers away. Despite decades of research, many questions about Stonehenge remain unanswered. Archaeologists are still uncertain exactly how the stones were transported and lifted into place using only the tools available at the time. Nor is it entirely clear whether tracking time was the monument's primary purpose or simply one function among several, including religious or ceremonial ones. What is clear is that Stonehenge represents an extraordinary achievement of ancient engineering and astronomical observation, one that continues to attract both scientists and visitors from around the world.
