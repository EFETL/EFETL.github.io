1.The committee's decision to cancel the festival was met with ______ from local business owners who had invested heavily in preparations.

(A) gratitude
(B) outrage
(C) indifference
(D) relief
中譯：委員會取消這項節慶活動的決定，引發了當地企業主的＿＿＿，因為他們已經為籌備工作投入大量資金。 正解 (B) outrage（憤怒、強烈不滿）：句子說明企業主「已經為籌備工作投入大量資金」(had invested heavily in preparations)，因此當活動被取消時，他們的反應應是強烈的「憤怒、不滿」(outrage)，這與「投入大量資金後卻面臨損失」的處境完全吻合，outrage 表達一種強烈、激動的負面情緒反應。 (A) gratitude（感激）：gratitude 是正面、感謝的情緒，與「投入大量資金卻面臨活動被取消」這種令人沮喪、不滿的處境完全相反，邏輯不符。 (C) indifference（冷漠、不在乎）：indifference 表示「毫不在乎、無所謂」的態度，但企業主「已經投入大量資金」，活動被取消對他們而言有直接的經濟損失，不太可能表現出「冷漠」的反應，邏輯不符。 (D) relief（鬆一口氣、寬慰）：relief 表示「擔憂解除後的放鬆感」，但企業主投入資金籌備活動，活動被取消對他們是「壞消息」而非「好消息」，不會讓他們感到「寬慰」，語意矛盾。

2.The professor encouraged students to ______ their arguments with solid evidence rather than personal opinion.

(A) support
(B) abandon
(C) disguise
(D) postpone
中譯：這位教授鼓勵學生用紮實的證據來＿＿＿他們的論點，而非僅憑個人意見。 正解 (A) support（支持、支撐）：support their arguments with solid evidence（用紮實的證據來支持他們的論點）是常見且自然的搭配，support 表示「提供依據以鞏固」論點，與後文「rather than personal opinion」（而非僅憑個人意見）形成對比——「個人意見」缺乏依據，而「紮實的證據」則能「支撐」論點，語意通順。 (B) abandon（放棄）：abandon their arguments with solid evidence 語意不通，「用紮實證據來放棄論點」邏輯矛盾——若有紮實證據，通常是用來「強化」而非「放棄」論點。 (C) disguise（偽裝、掩飾）：disguise their arguments with solid evidence 語意不通，「用證據來偽裝論點」邏輯不合，disguise 表示「隱藏真實樣貌、假裝成別的樣子」，與「提供證據以增強論點說服力」的教育情境不符。 (D) postpone（延後）：postpone their arguments with solid evidence 語意不通，「用證據來延後論點」邏輯不合，postpone 表示「延後某事的發生時間」，無法與「arguments」（論點）這個受詞搭配出合理的語意。

3.After years of training, the dancer's movements had become so ______ that she no longer needed to think about each step.

(A) clumsy
(B) automatic
(C) reluctant
(D) temporary
中譯：經過多年的訓練後，這位舞者的動作變得如此＿＿＿，以致於她不再需要思考每個步驟。 正解 (B) automatic（自動的、不經思考的）：so automatic that she no longer needed to think about each step（如此不經思考，以致於她不再需要思考每個步驟）語意通順，automatic 表示「不需主動思考、出於本能或習慣即可完成」的狀態，這正是經過多年訓練後，動作已內化成「本能反應」的描述，與後文「no longer needed to think」（不再需要思考）完全呼應。 (A) clumsy（笨拙的）：so clumsy that she no longer needed to think 語意不通，clumsy（笨拙）通常表示動作不協調、容易出錯，這與「多年訓練後」應有的「熟練、流暢」的結果方向相反，且「笨拙」與「不需思考」之間沒有合理的因果關係。 (C) reluctant（不情願的）：so reluctant that she no longer needed to think 語意不通，reluctant 是描述「人對某事的態度」（不情願），用來形容「movements」（動作）這個受詞本身的特質不恰當，且「不情願」與「不需思考」之間沒有邏輯關聯。 (D) temporary（暫時的）：so temporary that she no longer needed to think 語意不通，temporary（暫時的）表示「短暫、非永久」，這與「經過多年訓練」所形成的「穩定、持久」的技能狀態相矛盾，且與「不需思考」之間沒有合理因果關係。

4.The company issued a public apology after its advertisement was criticized for being culturally ______.

(A) insensitive
(B) admirable
(C) accurate
(D) generous
中譯：這家公司在其廣告被批評為文化上＿＿＿之後，發布了公開道歉。 正解 (A) insensitive（不敏感的、欠缺體察的）：criticized for being culturally insensitive（因文化上欠缺體察而受到批評）語意通順，culturally insensitive 是常見搭配，指「對其他文化的價值觀、習俗或敏感議題缺乏應有的尊重與體察」，這正是廣告內容引發爭議、導致公司「發布公開道歉」(issued a public apology) 的合理原因。 (B) admirable（值得讚賞的）：criticized for being culturally admirable 語意矛盾，若廣告因「值得讚賞」而受到批評，公司不會因此「道歉」，admirable 是正面評價，與「被批評」(criticized) 及「道歉」(apology) 的負面語境完全相反。 (C) accurate（準確的）：criticized for being culturally accurate 語意不通，若廣告內容「準確」呈現某文化，通常不會引發需要道歉的爭議，accurate 與「被批評、需道歉」的負面情境不符。 (D) generous（慷慨的）：criticized for being culturally generous 語意不通且矛盾，generous（慷慨）是正面特質，無法解釋為何廣告會「受到批評」並導致公司「公開道歉」。

5.Scientists warn that the ______ of plastic waste in the ocean poses a serious threat to marine ecosystems.

(A) shortage
(B) accumulation
(C) reduction
(D) removal
中譯：科學家警告，海洋中塑膠廢棄物的＿＿＿對海洋生態系統構成嚴重威脅。 正解 (B) accumulation（累積、堆積）：the accumulation of plastic waste in the ocean poses a serious threat（海洋中塑膠廢棄物的累積對...構成嚴重威脅）語意通順，accumulation 指「隨時間不斷增加、堆積」的過程，塑膠廢棄物在海洋中「持續累積」正是其對生態系統構成「威脅」的根本原因，accumulation 與 poses a serious threat（構成嚴重威脅）形成合理的因果關係。 (A) shortage（短缺）：the shortage of plastic waste in the ocean poses a serious threat 語意不通，「塑膠廢棄物的短缺」反而應該是「好事」（代表污染減少），不會「對海洋生態系統構成嚴重威脅」，邏輯矛盾。 (C) reduction（減少）：the reduction of plastic waste in the ocean poses a serious threat 語意矛盾，「塑膠廢棄物的減少」是正向的環境改善，不應「構成威脅」，與句子強調的「警告、威脅」的負面語境相反。 (D) removal（移除）：the removal of plastic waste in the ocean poses a serious threat 語意不通，「移除塑膠廢棄物」是清理環境的正向行動，不應該「構成威脅」，與句意方向相反。

6.The novel's ______ plot kept readers guessing until the very last page.

(A) predictable
(B) intricate
(C) shallow
(D) repetitive
中譯：這部小說＿＿＿的情節讓讀者一直猜測，直到最後一頁。 正解 (B) intricate（錯綜複雜的、精巧的）：an intricate plot that kept readers guessing until the very last page（錯綜複雜的情節讓讀者一直猜測到最後一頁）語意通順，intricate 形容情節「結構複雜、層層交織」，這樣的情節設計才能讓讀者「持續猜測」(kept...guessing) 故事發展，直到最後一頁才揭曉真相，與句意完全吻合。 (A) predictable（可預測的）：a predictable plot that kept readers guessing 語意矛盾，若情節是「可預測的」，讀者應該能輕易猜到後續發展，不會「一直猜測到最後一頁」，predictable 與「kept readers guessing」的描述完全相反。 (C) shallow（膚淺的）：a shallow plot that kept readers guessing 語意不通，「膚淺的情節」通常缺乏深度與懸念，難以讓讀者「持續猜測」至最後一頁，shallow 與「引人入勝、令人猜測」的描述不符。 (D) repetitive（重複的）：a repetitive plot that kept readers guessing 語意矛盾，「重複的情節」意味著內容一再重複、缺乏新意，讀者通常能預期接下來的發展，無法「持續猜測」，repetitive 與「kept readers guessing」的描述方向相反。

7.Local authorities have decided to ______ the old factory rather than tear it down completely.

(A) renovate
(B) abandon
(C) demolish
(D) ignore
中譯：地方政府已決定＿＿＿這座舊工廠，而非將其完全拆除。 正解 (A) renovate（翻新、修繕）：decided to renovate the old factory rather than tear it down completely（決定翻新這座舊工廠，而非將其完全拆除）語意通順，renovate 表示「修繕、改造以使其重新可用」，與後文「rather than tear it down completely」（而非完全拆除）形成對比——「翻新」與「拆除」是兩種互斥的處理方式，政府選擇了「翻新」這個保留舊建築的做法。 (B) abandon（放棄、廢棄）：decided to abandon the old factory rather than tear it down completely 語意不通，「放棄」與「拆除」並非邏輯上的對立選項——若工廠被「放棄不用」，仍然可能後續被拆除，abandon 無法與「rather than tear it down」形成有意義的對比。 (C) demolish（拆除、摧毀）：decided to demolish the old factory rather than tear it down completely 語意矛盾且重複，demolish 和 tear it down 都是「拆除」的意思，兩者意義相同，無法構成 rather than（而非）的對比關係，語意邏輯不通。 (D) ignore（忽視）：decided to ignore the old factory rather than tear it down completely 語意不通，「決定忽視這座舊工廠，而非將其完全拆除」邏輯怪異——「忽視」並非政府對工廠採取的「處理方式」，無法與「拆除」形成合理對比。

In 1958, a Swiss engineer named George de Mestral filed a patent for an invention that would eventually become a household staple: the hook-and-loop fastener, commonly known by its brand name, Velcro. The idea for the invention came to de Mestral after a walk in the Swiss countryside, during which he noticed that small burrs from plants had become stuck to his clothing and his dog's fur. Curious about why they clung so stubbornly, he examined the burrs under a microscope and discovered that they were covered in tiny hooks that latched onto the loops of fabric and fur. Inspired by this natural mechanism, de Mestral spent years experimenting with different materials to recreate the effect artificially. He eventually settled on nylon, which could be woven and then heat-treated to form thousands of tiny hooks on one strip of fabric, paired with a second strip covered in soft loops. When pressed together, the two strips formed a strong but easily reversible bond. Initially, Velcro was met with skepticism. Many people associated it with cheap, low-quality clothing, and it took years for the product to gain widespread acceptance. A major turning point came when NASA began using Velcro in its space program during the 1960s. Astronauts used it to secure tools, food packages, and equipment in the zero-gravity environment of spacecraft, where loose objects could otherwise drift away and cause problems. Following its association with space exploration, Velcro's reputation improved dramatically, and it began appearing in a wide range of products, from shoes and bags to medical devices and sports equipment. Today, the underlying technology continues to inspire new applications, demonstrating how observations of the natural world can lead to inventions that quietly become part of everyday life.

For most of human history, maps were drawn by hand, based on the observations and measurements of explorers, surveyors, and cartographers. These maps, while valuable, often contained significant errors, especially in regions that were difficult to access or had not been thoroughly explored. Coastlines might be drawn in the wrong shape, mountain ranges might be missing entirely, and the relative distances between cities were sometimes wildly inaccurate. The development of satellite technology in the second half of the twentieth century transformed mapmaking in ways that early cartographers could hardly have imagined. Satellites equipped with cameras and sensors could capture images of the entire planet's surface, including remote deserts, dense forests, and polar regions that had rarely, if ever, been mapped accurately before. These images allowed researchers to create maps with a level of precision and detail that would have taken centuries to achieve through ground surveys alone. Beyond simply showing the shapes of landmasses, satellite data has enabled scientists to track changes over time. By comparing images taken years or even decades apart, researchers can observe the retreat of glaciers, the expansion of cities, the loss of forests due to logging, and the changing course of rivers. This ability to monitor change has become especially important for understanding the effects of climate change, as scientists can now measure how quickly ice sheets are shrinking or how rapidly coastlines are eroding. Satellite mapping has also become deeply embedded in everyday life. Navigation apps used by millions of people rely on satellite data to provide real-time directions, while farmers use satellite images to monitor crop health and plan irrigation. What was once a tool reserved for governments and the military is now accessible to anyone with a smartphone, representing one of the most significant shifts in how humans understand and interact with the surface of their own planet.

In the highlands of Ethiopia, farmers have long faced a difficult challenge: how to grow enough food on steep, rocky slopes where soil is easily washed away by heavy seasonal rains. For generations, this erosion has made it harder for communities to produce stable harvests, often leading to food shortages during dry periods. In recent years, however, a relatively simple and low-cost solution has helped transform many of these hillsides: the construction of terraces. Terracing involves cutting a series of flat, step-like platforms into a hillside, often reinforced with stone walls or rows of plants along the edges. These structures slow the flow of rainwater down the slope, giving it time to soak into the soil rather than rushing away and carrying valuable topsoil with it. Community-based terracing projects have been organized in numerous villages, often relying on local labor rather than expensive machinery. Residents work together, sometimes for several weeks, to dig and shape the terraces by hand. While the initial labor investment is significant, the long-term benefits have been substantial. Farmers report that terraced land retains moisture far better than untreated slopes, allowing crops to survive longer dry spells. In some areas, terracing has also made it possible to grow crops on land that had previously been considered too steep or degraded for farming. Beyond agricultural benefits, terracing projects have had social effects as well. Because the work requires cooperation among many households, some communities report that the shared effort has strengthened relationships between neighbors and created a stronger sense of collective responsibility for managing local land. Researchers studying these projects caution, however, that terraces require ongoing maintenance; without regular repairs, walls can collapse and the benefits can be lost within a few years. Even so, for many highland communities, terracing has provided a practical, community-driven way to adapt to a challenging environment and improve food security for the future.
